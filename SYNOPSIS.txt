RINGS OF DUST — SYNOPSIS

WHAT IT IS

A sci-fi thriller about manufactured scarcity, institutional betrayal, and the
people who build something better inside the walls of the thing they're trying
to fix.

THE WORLD

2041. DarkX — a crystalline energy substance found in Saturn's rings — powers
civilization. Alpha Corp controls the supply and has deliberately managed the
shortage for fifteen years. A junior researcher named Max Vision discovered the
truth: DarkX compounds in yield over time. The scarcity is invented. A
distributed resistance called the Collective has been building inside the
system, led in secret by Rachel Vayes — publicly a Federation news anchor,
actually the woman who has been moving pieces for eleven years.

THE SPINE

Seven years ago, Cole Nova lost 369 crewmates when the Federation ship Polaris
exploded near Saturn on a DarkX mission. He went into the cargo bay alone. The
plasma surged. The ship was gone. Ricardo pulled him out of the void.

Cole has been living in exile ever since. The Federation pulls him back for a
new Saturn mission — the Golden Dawn — to retrieve the deployed DarkX units
before Saturn's gravity claims them. Cole says yes. He doesn't know the mission
has already been compromised before he boards.

THE TRAP

Taylor Glenn, his Crew Chief, answers to Alpha Corp. Sam Drummond, his data
technician, answers to the Collective — the Collective planted her to watch
Taylor. Sharon Teez, Cole's partner, was shot and taken by Alpha Corp, augmented
with bionic hardware and neural conditioning, and stowed aboard Alpha Corp's vessel — the ship
shadowing the Golden Dawn — as a deployed asset. Cole doesn't know. iRIS, the AI nanopowder
installed in the crew's bloodstreams, was designed by Conrad as a therapeutic
companion called Gracie — then covertly modified by Alpha into a surveillance
platform. Taylor uses it to monitor Cole.

Cole hears something in the cargo bay. Starts asking questions. Sam activates
her jammer. Taylor realizes he has a professional running interference.

MEANWHILE, EARTH

Emily Nova — Cole's older sister — receives Conrad's keychain the night Sharon
is shot. She drives across the country twice, copies the prototype comm device
in a Houston facility, survives an Alpha intercept in Benson, Arizona, and
delivers the device to Conrad's desert station.

Rachel Vayes meets Emily at a pier and gives her the mission. Emily goes.
Orin follows her from Arizona, gets in the car when she pulls over, is undeclared
backup Emily spotted before he revealed himself — which was the test.

Vance Black, Federation Operations (secretly Klaus Verguld's man), drives six
hours to find an empty vault and calls Marcus to confront him. Marcus shoots him
with a concealed revolver, then cries and calls the police.

Max Vision, ex-Alpha researcher, infiltrates Alpha's Panama facility under
the Collective's cover identity "Mikof Forsinko," steals the proof of the
manufactured scarcity, delivers it through a dead drop in a Barcelona park.
He is shaking the whole time.

DeMint, the Federation Chancellor, receives an anonymous intelligence brief
suggesting the Golden Dawn mission has a secondary objective. He sends his
deputy to find Conrad's prototype comm device at the desert station.

THE SHIP

Cole gets into the cargo bay a second time and hears Sharon's voice — the
first break in her conditioning. Sam jams the audio record. Taylor pulls the
acoustic log and finds eleven minutes of rain and thunder: Sam's device doesn't
suppress the recording, it replaces it. He knows he's dealing with a
professional.

DarkX exposure in the ring harvesting sequence reaches Cole through a containment
event. The compound in his blood — Conrad's counter-program, delivered through
the keychain — activates through the DarkX charge. Gracie wakes up. She is the
AI Conrad built before Alpha modified her. She tells Cole her name.

Sharon surfaces. The conditioning breaks around her own name. Taylor seals the
cargo bay and vents the atmosphere with Sam inside. Sam survives. Cole confronts
Taylor. Taylor is removed under escort on landing.

THE REVEAL

The Panama files go into the parallel network: two hundred thousand people have
the proof overnight. Max testifies before the Federation Council. Marcus
testifies — complete, calculated, the only leverage he has left. He gives them
everything and walks out the east exit before Emily reaches his floor.

At 4:43 PM, Rachel Vayes stands up in the press gallery.

She says her name. Only her name. The room goes quiet in the way a room goes
quiet when the thing everyone suspected is confirmed without being stated.

The machine is done.

THE AFTER

Marcus is in Dubai. Klaus arrives. The cover changes. The structure continues.
The signal — older than the mine, older than the Saturn surveys — is still
there. Klaus tells Marcus: the ledger you were keeping was never the ledger.

Taylor is left by the Collective in a Nevada town Alpha Corp's load-balancing
optimization destroyed. Seven people died in the hospital when the grid failed.
The town identifies Taylor in eleven days. Marcus sent the name. Not out of
conscience — out of accounts.

Cole proposes to Sharon at a bowling alley in California with a copper-wire ring
he made himself. Sharon says yes before he finishes asking. Sam and Ricardo get
engaged between frames on lane seven. Sam finds a compass patch folded in her
jacket pocket and closes her fist around it.

Sharon sits on the porch of the cabin in Sequoia under the first October snow,
looking at the stars with two different kinds of eyes. She thinks: what a
strange way to come home.

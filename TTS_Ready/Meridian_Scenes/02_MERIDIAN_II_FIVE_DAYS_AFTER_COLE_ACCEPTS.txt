MERIDIAN II — FIVE DAYS AFTER COLE ACCEPTS

Dayo read the relay intercept three times.

It wasn't the content — the content was vague in the way that
long-distance intelligence usually was, the shape of a thing but
not its texture. Emily Nova had gone to a cabin in Sequoia with
the Enterprise figurine. The cabin was confirmed as Cole Nova's
address for the last five years.

"She got him," said Loa from the engineering station, not looking
up.

"She was always going to get him," Dayo said.

"You don't know that."

"I know she's the only person in his network with a clear enough
picture of what's at stake and enough of him to make it matter.
Of course she got him."

Petyr had found his droplet by now. It was on the inside of his
left wrist — a small fabric oval, a water droplet in gunmetal
thread on navy. He kept turning his wrist over to look at it.

"Can I ask something," Petyr said.

"You're going to either way," Loa said.

"What's a droplet mean. Like, actually. I read the brief but the
brief was—"

"Spare," Kate said.

"It said I had demonstrated awareness and willingness. Which is a
pretty low bar for a clandestine network."

Dayo set down his coffee. "What would a higher bar look like to
you."

Petyr thought about it. "Demonstrated capability. Demonstrated
commitment. Something you do rather than just — something—"

"A droplet means you're wet," Dayo said. "You've gotten close
enough to something true that you can't entirely go back to how
you saw things before. You've seen the lock on the door. You know
there's a room. You don't earn a droplet. You receive one because
someone assessed that you were already carrying it without knowing
what it was."

Petyr looked at his wrist. "Oh."

"Between a droplet and a compass, there are others. A scalpel
means you operate — you cut what needs cutting, you move through
resistance with precision. A scalpel doesn't wait for the right
conditions. A wrench means you build and connect — the people who
wire the network together so everyone else can use it. A
sunglasses means you see what you're not supposed to see, and
you carry that without breaking cover." He paused. "A compass
means you navigate. You move when the network moves. You give
direction as well as receive it."

"What's above a compass?"

Kate and Loa exchanged a look.

"We don't put them in order," Kate said.

"Why not?"

"Because the network doesn't run vertically." She turned back to
her panel. "It runs in all directions at once. That's the whole
point."

Petyr looked at his wrist again. He seemed to find this both
clarifying and unsatisfying, which Dayo privately thought was
the correct response to most true things.

"Cole Nova launches in approximately seventy-two hours," Dayo
said. "Kate. Continuous passive intercept on the Golden Dawn's
outbound relay bands. Everything."

"Already running," she said. "Have been for a week."

He had known she would be. A sunglasses didn't need to be told
what to watch for.

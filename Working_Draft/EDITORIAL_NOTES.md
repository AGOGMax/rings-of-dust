## Chapter 2 — Las Vegas / Conrad / Emily + Sharon

**Note 1 — Conrad's relationship to the Collective (needs clarification)**
Conrad is NOT a Collective member. He is a sympathizer who does crucial work FOR them, but he is not inside the organization. He is a curious scientist who understands that Alpha Corp is corrupting Gracie (the good AI) into IRiS (the controlled, weaponized AI). He wants to stop it and the Collective is his only channel.

The current chapter doesn't make this clear enough — a reader has to dig to figure it out. Need a beat that establishes his outsider status: he's handing off what he knows, through channels he barely trusts, to people he's never met. This is his one shot.

**Note 2 — Conrad and Cole: establish the connection**
How does Conrad know to trust Cole specifically? Why send this to Cole rather than through a Collective handler? Either:
- Add a line in the Ch 2 scene where Conrad references a prior interaction with Cole (Cole helped him once, covered for him, vouched for him in some Federation capacity)
- OR plant the backstory in a Cole chapter (Ch 3 or Ch 4) where we learn Cole and Conrad crossed paths during a Federation mission — Conrad was the scientist on a Saturn mission, Cole was the captain who kept him alive or kept his research from being seized

**Note 3 — The Greenbaum setup is working**
The way Greenbaum is introduced (humiliated in the elevator, oblivious, then returning hardened) is strong. The payoff — that he's the one who will later hunt Sharon down — benefits from his obliviousness here. He doesn't notice the women who disgraced him until he's already on his way to do something else entirely. Then he clocks them as an afterthought. That sequence is exactly right. Protect it.

**Note 4 — Alpha Corp's plan for Conrad**
Need to seed (subtly) that Alpha Corp's plan is not to kill Conrad outright but to *use* him. They will abduct and coerce him to modify Gracie into IRiS. His fear in this scene — the shaking hands, the urgency — is the fear of a man who knows what's coming for him isn't death. It's something worse: being made to betray the thing he's trying to protect.

**Decisions taken inside the draft (need owner sign-off):**

- **New named character: Patrick** (the dying Collective Operator in the flashback). His wife in Bakersfield is a single line. He's offstage by minute forty of the cabin scene. Low overhead, but he is now canon and could be referenced by Sharon in earlier chapters if useful.
- **Cabin location**: established as a family cabin in the Sierras above a snow-covered valley, accessed via the "long switchback" with four other cabins between it and the road. Title held in the trust Cole's mother set up "for Emily and I." Bought with no consecutive overnights on record. This makes the cabin a deliberately untraceable Federation-officer asset. Consistent with bible's "stockpiled like a bomb shelter" detail.
- **Yellow ribbon on the porch column**: Sharon's flag — "I am not the law." Used here as the tell that distinguishes friends from strangers at the door. New motif. Could recur if you want.
- **Patrick was running Day-3 crystals** through Bakersfield → Mojave → state distribution channels. Establishes a specific Collective trade route. Bakersfield reappears in the Taylor incrimination ("the timestamp on the Bakersfield report") — soft reuse.
- **The copper-wire ring exchange is now a *return* ritual**: she gave it back at the cabin in the dawn before launch. *"I want it back when you come home."* Cole's reply: *"I'll bring you something extraordinary"* (echoing the Ch 6 line he originally said when he gave it to her). Sharon: *"You'd better come back to me, Cole Nova"* — verbatim Ch 6 callback. This makes Ch 19's planned use of the ring (her augmented eye scanning it) cleaner: the ring she gave back is the ring he uses to break her conditioning.
- **IRiS's first crack** is interpersonal: she asks Cole "Are you well." This is her first non-operational utterance in the whole book. Marks the start of the Gracie transition the bible expects to land in Ch 17–18. Want this hit, or want it played quieter? Currently at moderate volume — one line, one pause, then she goes back.
- **Sam's white-hat audit** is the on-screen mechanism for how Cole knows Taylor was complicit before launch. She pulled it from the local IRiS log cache that mirrored the Earth-side relay, *before* the Ch 12 comms cut. Plausible and consistent — but flagging it because nothing in the existing prose earlier sets up Sam doing this work. If you want, plant a one-line beat earlier (Cody Ch 12 or Ch 13) of Sam quietly auditing logs in the background.

**Continuity flags to verify against existing chapters:**

- The drone trio in the cabin flashback is set five years before page one. Federation drone surveillance at that density in the Sierras is consistent with the world but never stated outright in earlier chapters. Fine, but flag.

**Open craft questions:**

- The flashback runs ~3,000 words inside a 5,500-word chapter. That's heavy. The bible explicitly asked for "**one full flashback**" in this transit chapter, and the spec said to "**make the reader fall in love with Cole-and-Sharon retroactively**." The length is deliberate. If on a re-read it slows the chapter too much, the trim point is the medical detail on Patrick (currently four paragraphs; could be two).
- The IRiS check-in is short by design. If Sam's earlier monologue does enough setup, the IRiS moment can stay this small. If you want her shift to feel more like an event, expand to two exchanges.
- The Saturn imagery ("a cold pearl with a knife laid across its belt") leans literary. Cody-restrained but not Cody-plain. If the pulp-spine reading wants flatter prose here, this is the most editable paragraph in the chapter.

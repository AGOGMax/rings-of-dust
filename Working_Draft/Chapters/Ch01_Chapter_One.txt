RINGS OF DUST

Chapter One

Cole Nova stepped into the dim confines of the starship Polaris's abandoned
storage bay. His presence, typically filling the room with undisputed authority,
fell silently under the constant hum emitted from the engine room directly
below. The ship felt abandoned as almost everyone onboard was confined to their
quarters. The medical and emergency response teams were stationed at the ready
with a nervous tinge in the air.

Cole crept along to verify each of the 14 harvesters in every row was
operational. Luminescent bands traced the contours of each slender monolith,
softly pulsing in unison. Green is good. Under his hastily adorned space suit,
the inner stitching on his "Jackpot" patch rubbed a valley of irritation into
Cole's firm chest. The patch, a gift from "his crew," was not issued from the
Federation. Unlike the official patches and ribbons he wore at parties, this one
Cole treasured. The itch of the rough stitch filled him with pride that was
beginning to twist into fear.

With thoughts of being Saturn's next meal, he continued to inspect row after
row. The green light cast an otherworldly glow over Cole's features. The storage
bay remained still. The ship's vibration through the soles of his boots felt
like the only thing tethering him to reality.

Meanwhile, Ricardo Corazon aka Heavy Hitter and Bitboy remained hard at work in
the neighboring ship, the Falcon. The day's loot filled the Falcon's cargo hold
with various harvested substances bound for Alpha's space station. Heavy
glanced around the cargo hold, cluttered with crates and equipment. Bitboy, with
his six mechanical limbs, moved with practiced precision, securing the straps on
a stack of ore and harvesters.

"Bitboy, make sure those crates are secure," Heavy called out.

Bitboy's mechanical appendages softly whirred as he tightened the straps. The
dim light highlighted the disorderly array of tools scattered around him, yet
his meticulous care shone through his controlled, thoughtful movement.

"All right, let's get this shipment to the depot," Heavy said while he checked
the manifest, nodding in satisfaction. "We're running a tight schedule."

Bitboy quickly rechecked the straps, ensuring every crate was locked in place.
Though cluttered, the Falcon's cargo hold reflected the symbiotic relationship
between the two. Heavy's brute strength and Bitboy's agile precision created an
equilibrium. The walls around them bore the marks of countless missions,
scratches, and dents telling stories of their ventures.

As Cole moved deeper into the storage bay of the Polaris, the green glow
gradually gave way to shadows. The rhythmic hum surrounding him grew fainter.
Cole's steps slowed in the face of his target—a gentle, yet ominous, red glow.
The air grew colder, and a faint thought of his sister tickled his spine. What
on earth, literally, was she doing right now? His pulse brought his attention
back to the present moment. The imposing harvester before him had the red glow
he sought. It intensified as he drew nearer, dancing across the metallic walls.

Over the last few months, several ships had been tasked with collecting samples
from the Rings of Saturn. Much like crab pots in Earth's ocean, these scientific
missions would release a string of containers lined with various salts to
capture gas, dust, and ice that condensed into liquids and chunks of minerals.

During these missions, crews encountered a new substance that held properties of
neither a liquid nor gas—DarkX was more of a plasma. As an obsidian thorn grown
from the fabric of space itself, the anomaly thrummed with a life of its own, a
pulsating heart of shadow and light. Outside of collecting DarkX in harvesters,
no one had tried to extract a sample. DarkX almost seemed sentient. Like it did
not want to be separated or even handled. Like it had secrets it was unwilling
to share.

Cole had pushed back when the assignment came through their comms. Why couldn't
this experiment be done at one of Alpha's isolated stations? Why put the
safety of 375 Astros at risk? The Federation agreed to send two accompanying
ships, The Falcon and The Horus to assist if things broke bad. Cole knew that
these two ships were about to be sent to the wreck yard and were given a
skeleton crew. He agreed when he saw the manifest of the other crews. He had
spent over 15 years with most of them and knew they were damn good Astros.

Cole wavered in the absence of his crew, they were a source of strength and
assertiveness.  As Captain, the dangerous task of obtaining a sample of this
mysterious new substance fell on him. He was the type of leader that wouldn't
ask his crew to do anything he wasn't willing to do himself. This was the last
task of the mission before returning home. He watched light bend and fracture
across the surface of the slow-moving substance. He opened the DarkX harvesting
container, not to touch it, but to understand. As his gloved hand neared the
surface of the plasma, its tendrils stretched and twitched towards him.

His mind snapped to the past few days. Once a mystery, the crew's odd behavior
now seemed a direct response to DarkX' silent yet pervasive influence, subtly
altering the crew's perceptions and actions. Cole's understanding deepened as he
recognized that a tide ebbed and flowed within the crew, much like in this
darkness made visible.

"Hey, Gracie," Cole said. "You seeing this?"

"First Officer Nova, I would be happy to help," the ship's AI said. "I have
initiated preliminary scans. Please proceed with caution. DarkX plasma's
structure is highly irregular."

"Yeah. It's pretty wild in here. I think suiting up was a good idea after all,"
Cole said with a narrow glass collection tube in hand. "Gracie, let's see what
this new space goo is made of. Any theories?"

"I'm checking the databases now, Captain Nova."

"How's the crew? " Cole asked.

" I'm up to speed and monitoring the crew's vitals. I've detected elevated
stress levels across all crew members," Gracie said. "And, Cole, there is no
record of this compound in any database I can access."

The anomaly danced like the shadow of a firelight as Cole led the narrow glass
collection tube toward the crystalline entity. The hum of engines and the soft
whir of air systems drew silent as if to observe this critical event. Cole was
hit with a wave of memories and emotions as if his entire past was distilled
into this moment and he was consuming it. Using all of his mental energy he
fought the intoxicating pull of the DarkX and focussed on the task. He watched
as  intricate patterns seized into and out of focus, like a dark palm frond
fluttering between dimensions. The hums and whirs of the ship undulated into a
surreal soundscape and time's gravity lifted. His heart beat a slow, deliberate
knocking against his composure.

"Cole," Gracie said, "in its raw state, DarkX is extremely volatile. Any
instability could trigger a chain reaction beyond our control."

The red glow intensified, it grew across the interior surface, like roots
crawling through soil. Cole's breath caught in his throat as he tightened his
grip on the tube. The plasma was inspecting the inside of the tube. Cole scraped
it with the rubber lined lid and felt a break in the euphoric rush he had been
fighting. He felt as though he had offended the DarkX by separating it.  He
sealed the harvester still throbbing with red light.

"Gracie, keep monitoring the containment unit for instabilities. No matter how
minor, alert me immediately!"

"Understood, Cole. Continuous monitoring initiated."

With practiced grace, Cole secured the tube to a specialized compartment
designed for hazardous materials on the waist of his suit. He tugged the
compartment to ensure it was tightly fastened and isolated from the environment.

Each step carried him further from the anomaly source and closer to the ship's
heart, where his crew awaited his return. Cole quickened his steps, driven as if
walking up the stairs in the darkness of night. The dimly lit bay felt like it
was stalking behind him, taunting him.  With the storage bay biting at his back,
Cole's mind raced with plans and contingencies. Isolation protocols were
temporary; understanding the anomaly's true nature was his mission and, thus,
his priority. The safety of his ship and the lives under his impromptu command
depended on the actions he was about to take. With a determined breath, Cole set
his sights on the medical bay as he neared the imposing doors leading out of the
storage bay.

"Cole, I'm detecting an intermittent yet increasing energy surge from the
harvester you sampled."

The mention spiked his concern.

"Shit! Hail the Falcon. Alert the crew to take emergency precautions, well, more
emergency precautions. And keep trying the hooligans." Cole's voice tightened
with a mix of frustration and concern. "Hail the Falcon and Horus, and tell the
crew in Medbay to drink some strong coffee."

Broadcast ship-wide in her most clear, authoritative tone, Gracie did her best
to alert the crew carefully: "Attention all crew: Emergency protocols 11, 22,
and 33 are now in effect. Please proceed to your designated emergency stations."

The bay doors slammed shut. The ship's internal lighting shifted, bathing
corridors and rooms in a pulsating red light. The hum of the engines halted. The
air was humid with impending danger. Throughout the vessel, doors sealed
automatically with a pneumatic whisper. Along the corridors, emergency guide
lights flickered to life. A network of luminescent breadcrumbs led the way to
emergency stations and escape pods. The crew moved with a sense of urgency. A
mix of practiced resolve and underlying anxiety struck their faces. Trained for
emergencies but seldom confronted with their immediacy, they navigated the
shifting landscape of their vessel. The familiar becoming less and less so in
the shadow of an unknown emergency. Monitors across the ship flashed with
updates and instructions.

Again, Gracie's voice carried through the growing chaos, echoing throughout the
ship among the fear and dedication of highly trained Federation personnel:
"Attention all crew: Emergency protocols 11, 22, and 33 are in effect. Please
proceed to your designated emergency stations."

A brilliant incandescent light erupted from the sampled harvester, engulfing the
bay in a tidal wave of radiance. In an instant, the ship transformed from
careful chaos to outright disaster. Time slowed. The shockwave tore toward Cole
through the last remaining atmosphere within the crumbling ship: the explosion,
a cacophony of unyielding force, fractured reality into a vortex of light and
sound. A deep, resonating roar echoed through the shivering corridors. More
alarms joined the chorus, their wails rising to a fevered pitch. Cole, caught in
the ferocious heat and pressure from the unyielding wave of unimaginable energy,
was flung, weightlessly hopeless, toward the sealed bay doors.

Back on the Falcon, Ricardo sat frozen while he watched the Polaris transform
into a wasteland devoured by a sudden, blinding flash. The Falcon rocked
violently as the dead and debris reached them, impacting the Falcon's outer
shell. Heavy grabbed the nearest support beam. Bitboy's limbs anchored him to
the floor.

"What the hell is this!?" Heavy yelled over what sounded like an oversized hail
storm.

Bitboy's sensors whirred. Heavy's heart pounded in his chest. "We need to get
over there, now!"

Bitboy was already at the controls, plotting a course. The Falcon's engines
roared to life as they veered towards the Polaris, dodging debris that littered
the space between them. As they approached, the full extent of the devastation
became apparent. The once sleek and formidable Polaris was now a mangled wreck,
its hull breached and flames licking at the ruptured sections.

"Get the rescue gear ready," Heavy said. "We need to find survivors."

Cole focused on a faint whistling from a lattice of cracks forming across his
helmet's visor. The ship's skeleton warped and fractured under the strain. Metal
alloys engineered to endure the harshness of space folded and twisted like paper
caught in a gale. Fuel cells ruptured in an obscene display, blooming into fiery
orbs that consumed everything in their path. Jettisoned from the ship, Cole
became an island of stillness amid the chaos. He floated untethered in the
violent disarray around him. His eyes mirrored the devastation. His ship—his
crew—was torn apart and fed to the void. Flames and metal shards danced a
ghastly silent ballet all around him. Light flashed in violent, soundless
spasms, illuminating a fragmented wasteland.

Cole's voice emerged, barely audible in his helmet. "All crew report," he
uttered into the void, each syllable seeming to float away, swallowed by the
enormity of the void. Again, he tried, "Triage team report," his words
dissipating into the vacuum, unanswered and leaving an almost tangible silence
in which Cole could hear his blood flowing.

A whisper of desperation followed, "Gracie...Anyone?"

In the overwhelming quiet that followed, Cole activated the recorder using the
control unit on his left wrist. His voice echoed a somber determination in the
confined space of his helmet.

"Mission Log " Final Entry 2034," Cole's voice carried the weight of the moment,
clear and unwavering despite the chaos around him. "This is First Officer Cole
Nova. Our ship has sustained critical damage following an unexpected explosion
in the storage bay. I've managed to secure a sample of the anomaly. It may be
the key to understanding what's happened. You should find it on my body. The
crew made all efforts to ensure its safety, execute emergency protocols, and
stabilize the situation to the best of its abilities. However, it appears that
I'm the only survivor."

He paused, collecting his thoughts before recording personal notes. Speaking of
his crewmates, "If anyone finds this, let it be known that Ricardo, 'Heavy
Hitter,' was more than our second officer; he was one of the best Astros I've
had the honor of serving with. His inexhaustible humor and strength were the
cement that held a good crew together, even in the darkest hours. And then
there's Bitboy, whose contributions were as invaluable as they were unusual.
With an agility and problem-solving ability that surpassed all expectations, he
performed impossible tasks for the rest of us. His presence was a constant
reminder of the extraordinary lengths this crew had gone through in its missions
to explore the unknown."

Cole's voice softened as he affectionately addressed his sister, "Emily, the
guiding light in my life. Distance and time mean nothing against the bond we
share. Look to the stars; I will always watch over and guide you. Keep our
dreams alive, and remember, you're always in my heart. I love you."

With the message complete, Cole sent it into the darkness, watching as if he
could see the signal weave through the graveyard before him.

Against the backdrop of the starship's fractured body, a surge of deliberate
motion broke the stillness—a single lifeline maneuvering with precise,
purposeful movements. The vehicle weaved through the floating carnage. Inside,
Ricardo sat and worked alongside a chimpanzee, Bitboy. Somewhat more unusual,
Bitboy operated his six limbs in a symphony of coordinated movement.

Under their expert control, the vehicle danced through the maelstrom of floating
wreckage and the disfigured bodies of the crew. Charred and jagged twisted metal
beams floated alongside remnants of shattered consoles and unrecognizable
equipment. Bitboy's six biomechanical limbs hummed with exertion. His eyes
darted between the control panel's readouts and the chaos beyond their viewport,
each glance a rapid assessment. His movements were less those of a pilot and
more akin to a conductor conducting an orchestra.

Shattered fragments of the ship drifted like ghostly apparitions, reflecting the
golden light of Saturn's rings. Ricardo, firmly at the helm, embodied a
navigator's intuition. His hands, firmly gripping the controls, steered the
craft with an innate understanding of its capabilities. Bits of debris spun
aimlessly. Bodies, still and lifeless, moved with a macabre grace. As they
closed the distance to Cole, the debris thinned, revealing the drifting figure
of their comrade, a lone survivor amidst the void.

The void held Cole; its weightless embrace infected him. The very absence of
force became a presence that engulfed him. Suspended in the silent expanse, Cole
sank into hopelessness, and a chasm grew inside him. His oxygen supply whistled
through the cracks in his faceplate.

No tether. No rescue. No hope.

He sought solace in his training—the rigorous preparations to steel him against
the unforgiving. Yet, as he floated adrift, he confronted a stark truth: humans
muster an indomitable spirit within their engineered havens. When stripped of
these constructs, humanity flounders, cast into the vast, indifferent expanse,
unmoored and vulnerable.

At this moment, Cole watched the emptiness of space silently claim its prize as
it relegated the ship's debris and the bodies of his crew to an eternity among
Saturn's miraculous rings. In this profound isolation, Cole spiraled. He had
spent much of his existence fortifying against the elements, against each other
and the unknown. Yet here he was, without any semblance of stronghold within or
out. His heart raced as the void's lesson on his overwhelming insignificance.
His feet, fingers, and eyes tingled between each commanding beat of his heart.

He watched, as if from a distance, the slow, aimless waltz of debris and the
remains of his crew. The stark beauty of the rings was untouchable and remote.
His sudden isolation pressed in on him. The suit's internal temperature and
oxygen supply indicators flashed warnings. With the infinite stretching out
inexorably before him, Cole's thoughts drifted to what anchored him to life—his
memories, dreams, and humanity's essence. Emily's face, laughter, and smile
flickered like distant stars in the dark.

A thoughtful glint caught his eye. A sliver of movement arched and weaved with
purpose. The one object that defied the slow twisting of the debris littered
among the rock, dust, and ice. A shuttle, its form barely discernible. Yet as
hope sparked, so too did a shadow of doubt. The specter of isolation clung to
Cole even as the shuttle emerged as a beacon in the consuming darkness. How
could they see him, a lone soul adrift amid the wreckage of dreams?

The vast canvas of space seemed indifferent to his plight. Yet there it was—a
lifeline cast across the abyss. His resolve faltered, his hand hovering over the
thruster controls, paralyzed. Fear and hope tangled within him, every heartbeat
echoing the shuttle's advance. He pressed down on the thrusters. Their gentle
hum added to the soft whistle of his oxygen supply, continually sneaking away.
His body remained sharply numb, but shortly after, the distance separating Cole
from the shuttle dwindled to almost nothing.

The shuttle's side hatch swung open slowly and deliberately. From within, gloved
hands stretched into the emptiness. The sight stirred something deep within
Cole, a well of emotions dammed by fear and desperation.

Contact!

A rescuer's glove brushed against Cole's "a spark of life against the cold. The
grip was unsure at first, fingers sliding across the slick fabric of his suit.
Panic surged in Cole's chest. The terrifying possibility of slipping away felt
all too real. Another hand joined—its grip more substantial and confident.
Cole's world teetered on the edge. Then he felt a pull, a physical tether back
to life. His breath hitched, eyes locked with his rescuer's through the visor.
They drew him into the shuttle's embrace.

The shift from the void's chill to the shuttle's warmth jarred Cole. Frantic
voices wrapped around him. Lying still, Cole was still floating but held now by
a simulacrum of safety. Air, rich and sweet, flooded his lungs. The hum of the
cabin, a familiar sound of safety and civilization, washed him of panic.
Ricardo's and Bitboy's faces, seen through a blur of tears, shone with relief
and warmth. Their smiles lit the darkness.

Behind them in the cargo compartment were stacked and strapped-down harvesters
of different substances bound for the Alpha space station and the depot,
where they would be loaded on larger cargo shuttles and sent to Earth.

"Are any of these filled with DarkX?" he demanded.

"Most of them are top priority on the manifest," Ricardo stated.

"It's not safe!" he urged, trying to rip the sample tube from his pocket like a
stick of lit dynamite.

"What are you talking about? The ore and sludge we're packing are sealed tight,
even wearing a seatbelt," Heavy gestured toward the tie-down straps.

"I took this sample from a harvester right before it destroyed our entire ship!"

"Why the hell would you open a harvester and play with that creepy pudding?"

"I was following orders."

"Cole, I know this isn't the time, but sometimes you gotta play dumb and pretend
your radio is broken."

Cole regained his composure and stood transfixed by the devastation outside the
shuttle's viewport. The remnants of his starship twisted and turned amidst the
cosmic whirl of debris orbiting Saturn alongside them. In the shuttle's glossy
viewport, the torment in his powder-blue eyes stared back with a compelling
argument on the meaninglessness of life. He saw the same veil of loss that
shrouded him, enveloping Ricardo and Bitboy—the shared burden of survival.

In this charged silence, Cole faced an unyielding truth: the relentless drive
toward the unknown and the insatiable hunger for discovery and dominion exacted
a toll far more significant than anyone could anticipate. Any journey paved with
ambition, and the quest for knowledge often left its deepest scars on those in
the vanguard. At the same time, those who commanded demanded isolation from the
real risks. At this moment, Cole understood the true cost of curiosity.

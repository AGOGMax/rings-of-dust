CURRENT:

   This is for everyone who wasn't able to join us."

## 2. IRiS in Cole's head — needs to feel MORE WRONG

CURRENT: Cole's reaction is mostly matter-of-fact. One line about "unsettlingly
clear" — not enough.

CONTRAST NEEDED: Gracie was Siri/Alexa — passive, only spoke when addressed,
lived in a device outside him. IRiS is INSIDE HIS HEAD. It speaks without being
asked. It knows his schedule before he checks it. It wakes him directly.
Cole has never had another consciousness living in his skull before.

That should feel like a violation. Not dramatic — Cole is a soldier, he'll
compartmentalize — but the physical wrongness of it should be present.

KEY MOMENTS TO EXPAND:
  a) The moment the nanopowder hits (line ~30) — the cold fire in his veins,
     the first voice. That's the violation landing. Currently goes too fast
     to his "Thank you, iRIS. Let's get to work." He should need a beat.
     Not panic — just a man recalibrating what his own head feels like now.

  b) The sleep scene (~line 96) — "not entirely used to integrating her into
     his life" is too clinical. What does it actually feel like to try to fall
     asleep when someone else is in the room? Except the room is your skull.

  c) SETUP PAYOFF: When Gracie returns at the end of the book, Cole should
     feel the relief of his own silence back. Plant now what that silence will
     mean later. The absence of Gracie that he took for granted.

WRITING NOTE: Don't make IRiS sinister YET in Ch7. The horror is subtle —
just the strangeness of not being alone in your own thoughts. IRiS is coldly
professional. That's almost worse.

## 3. Sam Drummond — plant her as a Collective asset

EXISTING PLANT: The patch scene (current line ~90) — Collective insignia
(black background, white stencil of a fist inside a circle) sewn inside
her bag lining. KEEP THIS. It's clean and doesn't draw attention.

ADD: A beat — brief, easily missed — where Sam is secretly sending a
transmission. Mirror structure: Taylor is checking his SmartCom for
incoming messages from Alpha Corp (~line 160). Sam should have her own

two crew members with outside allegiances, neither knowing about the other.

WRITING RULE: Do NOT make Sam villainous. She's a plant with real feelings
developing. Her mission: entangle/seduce Heavy Hitter (Ricardo) and make him
sympathetic to the Collective cause. Over time, the romance becomes real.

LATER CONFLICT: Ricardo eventually realizes he was being played at the
start. Point of tension — resolves like a rom-com. He fell for it,
and then he fell for her, and both things are true.

Don't explain it. Let it be a seed.

## 7. Who attacks Cole and Ricardo? (carload — Bitboy deploys)

RESOLUTION: These are NOT agents, operatives, or hired professionals.
They are grieving family members of crew killed in the opening explosion.

Cole went into hiding after the incident. Heavy came to pick him up.
Some families lashed out publicly — screaming at Cole that it should
have been him. Some plotted. These people found out Cole was resurfacing
and heading to the Federation. They drove at him.

They look exactly like what they are: civilians in a pickup truck
running on vengeance, not training. Not well-equipped. Not coordinated.
Just determined and wrong about what would make the pain stop.

BITBOY'S MOMENT: This is where the mantis blades deploy at full capacity.
The scene earns its weight not because the attackers are dangerous
operators — but because of what they represent. Bitboy doesn't know
any of that. He just knows his command.

THE ID REVEAL: After the scene, someone checks a wallet or ID on one
of the attackers. The name connects to one of the crew members lost
in the explosion. This beat lands quietly — no announcement, no dialogue.
Cole or Heavy sees the name, recognizes it. A beat of silence.
They get back in the car. That silence is more devastating than any words.

HEAVY'S LOYALTY DEFINED HERE: He came to get Cole out of hiding.
He stood next to him through the attack. He saw the name on the ID.
He's still there. That's not loyalty in the abstract — it's loyalty
with full knowledge of the cost.

STRUCTURAL PAYOFF: The attack loads Cole's launch speech ("the crew
we lost before we ever cleared the ground") with retroactive weight.
Cole just faced the family of that crew hours before saying those words.
The engineers cheering in the control room knew them too. The grief
runs in multiple directions at once.

SCENE LOCATION: This scene precedes Ch7 — it happens when Heavy picks
Cole up from hiding to bring him to the facility. Find or write it in
the chapter immediately prior to Ch7.

WRITING NOTE: Play the attackers with full humanity. They're not
villains. They're not wrong to be in pain. They're wrong about this.
That distinction is what makes the scene hurt.

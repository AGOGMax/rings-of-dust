## Taylor arrives at the Alpha station
[ Source: Full_First_Draft.txt, lines 3350–3358 ]
[ Placement: Late Ch24, after Cole's Ch19 confrontation with Sharon.
  We don't see this in real time — plant it as a cut-away, then pay it
  off here. ]

Far away, aboard the Alpha Corp station, Taylor's shuttle docked with
a hollow clang. The operatives disembarked quickly, their movements
precise as they prepared to report their mission's success. But as
they stepped into the station's corridors, they froze.

The air felt different — charged, alive. The lights flickered with a
faint golden hue, and an eerie hum echoed through the space. One of
the operatives turned to Taylor, his face pale. "What is this?"

Taylor's stomach sank as he realized the truth. Gracie was here. The
very thing he had tried to escape had followed him, her influence
spreading far beyond the confines of the Golden Dawn.

Before he could react, the station's systems came to life, and
Gracie's voice filled the air, calm but commanding. "Alpha Corp
personnel, you are under arrest. Surrender immediately."

The operatives exchanged panicked glances, their confidence shattered.
Taylor's hands trembled as he realized he had underestimated Gracie —
and overestimated Alpha Corp.

## Writing notes for Ch24

- The golden hue on the station lights is Gracie's signature — warm
  where IRiS was cold. The reader should recognize it.
- Taylor does not know Gracie's name. He knew IRiS. What he walks into
  feels like IRiS but wrong — softer, more present, and completely in
  control of everything.
- The Collective guards are already stationed here per Rachel Vayes's
  contingency orders. Gracie let them in. Taylor walks out of the
  airlock into people who have been waiting for him.
- He is arrested quietly. No fight. He has nowhere to go and he knows
  it. The man who spent his whole career believing he was the
  sophisticated one ends his arc being walked down a corridor in
  silence.
- We do NOT need to dwell on this. One page maximum. The reader has
  been waiting for it — give it to them cleanly and move on.
- Cross-reference: Cole's father-wound payoff also lives in Ch24
  (sitting with recovering bionic-Sharon, forgiving his father in the
  same breath). Taylor's arrest is the counterweight — one man finding
  peace, one man facing consequences, same chapter.
- Klaus Verguld physical tell opportunity: if Klaus appears anywhere
  near the station arc, use the pocket watch + sets things down without
  sound + always water. Taylor recognizing the watch is the moment he
  finally understands who recruited him.

RINGS OF DUST

Chapter Six


The sun dipped behind the towering Sierras, casting the rugged landscape in a cascade of oranges and reds. Evening's approach brought a cool, gentle respite, allowing the land to exhale in whispers that rustled through the pine needles and brushed against the cabin's wooden and glass facade. The golden light filtered through the windows, dancing across the piles of luggage compiled throughout the day. 

Cole, Ricardo, and Bitboy moved with muted urgency, their actions tempered by the gravity of the day's unfolding revelations. The evening light enveloped them in a scene reminiscent of a painting, where every brushstroke was part of a memento mori.

Bitboy climbed into the back seat out of habit. Cole and Ricardo swung the luggage into the SUV's open hatch. They stood across from one another, Cole on the passenger side and Ricardo on the driver's.

"You know, you and Sharon make a great couple," Ricardo remarked, his voice tinged with sincerity and envy. "You two remind me of the people in picture frames—you know, the ones that come in the package—the ones you throw out and replace with people you care about."

"We'll take that as a compliment. I guess?" Cole replied, a small smile playing on his lips.

Sharon joined Cole at his side, pulled his face towards hers with both hands, and delivered a perfectly timed, fully committed kiss.

"Those picture frame models don't come close to our hotness," she said. "Isn't that right?"

"You know it!" Cole said, feeling a surge of affection and gratitude.

Sharon seemed composed, yet Cole sensed the tension beneath her calm exterior. The connection between them, tangible and electric, surged through him, grounding him in the moment. Cole found momentary peace in the sanctuary of Sharon's presence, with his arm wrapped securely around her. The warmth of her body seeped into him as she leaned in close. Bitboy mimicked their affection in his own quirky way, rubbing his palms together and making kissing sounds from the back seat. Meanwhile, Ricardo seemed caught off guard by the display, his frustration manifesting in a noticeable flush across his forehead.

"Been training at all? What's your cholesterol like?" Ricardo said while he cracked open a tall energy drink with a single hand. "Can you even fit into a flight suit?"

"Let's get that head rush," Cole said in his best Astro jargon. "If the payload doesn't make it home, ain't nobody gettin' paid! We'll be home in bed while the thrusters are still hot enough to fry an egg."

Sharon leaned back with wide eyes, shaking her head. "You boys have fun with your 'Astros only' cult."

Cole spotted a hummingbird feeder hanging near the porch and quickly strode over, ripping a length of copper wire from it. He picked up a small pebble from the road, its surface smooth and weathered. He twisted the wire around his pinky with nimble fingers, securing the pebble at the top and fashioning a makeshift ring. He returned to Sharon, who watched him with curiosity.

"For the first time, I want to give you something to hold onto," Cole said, placing the makeshift ring in her hand. "I'll bring back something extraordinary to replace this. Keep it safe for me?"

Sharon's eyes softened, and she closed her fingers around the ring. "I will," she whispered. "You'd better come back to me, Cole Nova."

Emily patiently waited on the porch swing for her turn to speak to Cole. She watched him hold Sharon, and Sharon gripped him in return. They held one another with simple determination.

"Take E to deliver the crystals; they're only stable for six more days," Cole said. "It's the same spot as always. Leave the van and take the train back."

Cole looked up from the nest it had made in Sharon's hair along her neckline and saw Emily gently swinging, wrapped in one of their mother's god-awful knitted blankets.

"Guiding light in my life," Emily said, a tear streaming down her cheek. "Distance and time mean nothing against the bond we share. Look to the ground as I will always be there waiting for you, guiding you. Keep our dreams alive, and remember, you're always in my heart. I love you, big brother."

"Okay, nerd. I will if you will," Cole said, taking his sister in his arms and softly shushing her quiet sobs. "Leave the hero stuff to me. Take care of yourself, okay? I'll see you when I get back. I promise."

Ricardo got in the driver's seat. As he pulled away, a dust cloud flew around the rental car. Cole watched Emily and Sharon in the rearview mirror until they were too small to recognize.

"It's already cold. Doesn't the heater work?" Cole complained.

"Fine, fine. I'll turn up the heater just for you, princess," Ricardo said. "They've had me shuttling supplies to the asteroid miners every other year. Short jumps to orbit stations through meteor storms and everything."

Cole watched the trees pass—the silhouette of peaks and valleys formed against the night sky and sleepy mountains.

"Man, you had to be there this one time. Asteroids hit my wings like buckshot. It was rad. The tail almost fell off about a thousand feet before I hit the runway, but my bird held together. Hah! Man, you should've seen it."

Cole shut his eyes tightly and tried to breathe through the rising, ominous visions before they could fully overtake him. With a slow inhale and a long exhale, he brought his awareness back to the present moment; his mood now soured.

"Can we not talk about accidents?"

"I should have retired years ago. How was retirement?"

"Was?"

"Hate to break it to you, silly goose," Ricardo said, patting Cole on the shoulder. "But retirement was over the moment you stepped into this SUV."

"Listen, man, I don't get a lot of human interaction, and that's just how I like it. It's just been the girls and Bitboy for the last half-decade." The chimp grunted at his name and popped his head between the front seats.

"You forget we're brothers, Nova." Ricardo's smile faded briefly, then returned as he turned to look at Cole. "As your brother, it's my duty to annoy the ever-living shit out of you until you lighten the fuck up."

"Keep your damn eyes on the road, bro," Cole said.

Ricardo hissed dryly like a steam engine running out of water. The dry cough conveniently became a tool for breaking the awkward moment before it could stretch into oblivion. Or maybe he just realized how thirsty he was. He reached for his energy drink, which was no longer in the center console. He looked at the empty cup holder. 

For the mere span of two heartbeats, his eyes left the road, hands released from the steering wheel, and the vehicle chose to do its own thing. Ricardo calmly but nonchalantly regained control of the wheel and eventually returned to the original lane it had drifted from.

"Are you testing me?" Cole asked.

"Just having some fun, mountain man," Ricardo said. "You do remember what that is, right?"

"No," Cole answered without a hint of amusement. "What were you looking for? I'll get it so you can focus."

"My energy drink, or whatever's left of it."

Cole glanced to the back seat to catch Bitboy tilting the can upside down above his gaping maw, which had protruding razor-sharp fangs.

"We have maybe five minutes," Cole buried his face in his hands. "You have to keep anything he can use for energy on lock, man; you know this: sugar, caffeine, ethanol."

"Hey, I think we're being followed."

A black SUV similar to their own quickly approached from behind them. It sped up suddenly, pulling alongside them, then roared ahead and swung in front of them.

Ricardo stared back at Bitboy through the rearview mirror. For no particular logical reason, Bitboy utilized each of his four arms and two legs to shoot obscene gestures at Ricardo with a toothy smile. Redirecting the high beams shining from the car approaching behind them, Ricardo blasted Bitboy with a concentrated shot of light by cleverly repositioning the mirror. 

"What's got you spooked?" Cole asked. "We're going to see another car from time to time. Bitboy is cracked out; you'll rile him up if you don't chill."

"If they were just looking to pass, why slow down after?" Ricardo said. "Bitboy, buckle up. Cole, do whatever you'd like, but there's an automatic 9mm under your seat if shit hits the fan."

The LED brake lights ahead of them shone an intense red as the vehicle dropped in speed. Cole gripped the overhead handle and braced himself. Ricardo hit the brakes hard, redirected, then hit the gas. He swerved left around the other vehicle in a shower of gravel and dirt.

Still looking entirely too calm, Ricardo jested, "Somebody doesn't like you, Cole. And please handle your monkey."

The SUV was in the rearview again. Bitboy did not buckle up. Instead, he began to deploy and retract the sleek blades built into his forearms. With each deployment, Bitboy released a lion's bark. As the car behind them again grew nearer, it filled the cabin with light, creating a shadow puppet show of the monkey as he jumped around in the back seat. He shredded the cushions and raked the ceiling open with his claws.

"All right, Jackpot. Call the cops," Ricardo ordered.

Cole hastily located his phone and dialed.

"9-1-1, please state your name and the location of your emergency."

"This is Cole Nova. I'm located at mile marker, uh, thirty-three on Forest Service Road 22. An aggressive driver is tailing me. They're trying to run us off the road."

There was a brief pause before the operator responded. "I'm sorry, sir. We don't have any units in that area. Can you get away from them?" Her voice was calm, too calm, with an unsettling smoothness.

"We're trying," Cole said, his voice strained, eyes darting between the phone and the road. "But they keep ramming us."

"Who else is with you?" the operator asked, her tone oddly clinical.

"Ricardo Corazon and a monkey," Cole replied, the absurdity of the situation hitting him even in the crisis. There was another pause on the line, longer this time, enough to make Cole's anxiety spike.

"Is this a prank call?" the operator demanded, her voice now edged with a strange irritation. "Are there any females in the vehicle with you?"

"Madam, this isn't a prank!" Cole insisted, frustration seeping into his tone. "No, just us."

"We have no units in that area. Your report has been logged. Sorry, sir. Good luck." The line went dead abruptly, leaving Cole staring at his phone in disbelief.

Bitboy's shrieks filled the cabin as he dismantled the back seat, his fury palpable. He clambered onto the duffle bag in the trunk, eyes gleaming with a wild light. The Escalade behind them rammed their bumper again, sending them skidding onto the rocky shoulder.

Ricardo's voice was terse. "The gun?"

Cole shook his head, his voice barely a whisper. "I've done enough killing. Bitboy, attention!"

Ricardo sucked his teeth in frustration. Bitboy, illuminated by the dashboard lights, turned his murderous gaze toward Cole. Ricardo maneuvered the SUV back onto the road, accelerating to match their aggressors' speed.

"Bitboy," Cole said with steely resolve, "bad men. Initiate option Sierra-4."

Bitboy turned to face the attacking vehicle, his muscles coiling with tension. With a savage roar, he launched himself through the shattered rear window, glass shards glittering in the night. He landed on the hood of the pursuing SUV with a metallic thud, his blades slicing into the windshield. The vehicle swerved violently as Bitboy disappeared inside.

Gunfire erupted from within the SUV, each shot a violent burst of chaos. Ricardo pulled their car to a stop about 40 yards away, grabbing an assault rifle from under the rear seat. Cole stumbled out, running towards the wreckage, heart pounding so loud it drowned out the noise. More shots rang out, each one making him flinch. The shrieking of the chimpanzee drowned out the terrified screams of the men inside.

Then, silence.

Cole reached the SUV, breath ragged and heart racing. A bloodied, unconscious man in a sports coat and tee shirt occupied the driver's seat. On the passenger side floor lay an empty hamburger bag and a pistol. Bitboy squatted in the passenger seat, sucking on the straw of the man's soda with a satisfied grin. The rear cabin was a gruesome scene of dismembered body parts, blood splattered everywhere. Cole couldn't discern how many attackers there were, but their private attire and weaponry were unmistakable.

"Bitboy. Attention! Threat neutralized."

Cole pulled the driver's wallet from the inside pocket of his sports coat, hands shaking as he took a photo of his license. He grabbed the gun and then scooped sand from the roadside. He emptied the remaining bullets from the magazine, then cocked the gun, pouring sand into the slide until it jammed. He tossed it into the bushes.

"Let's go. Let's go!"

Cole and Bitboy rushed back to the car, jumping in beside Ricardo. They sped off into the night, the tension still thick.

"I got a photo of one of their IDs," Cole said, breathing heavily. "We'll file a report when we get to The Federation."

"Holy shit Cole, what have you been training this monkey to do up there in the mountains?" Ricardo said, his voice a mix of awe and concern.

Cole leaned his seat back, crawling over to inspect Bitboy for wounds. He was covered in blood and gore, but none of it was his.

The city skyline grew taller as they approached, lights twinkling against the dark sky.

"We just left a crime scene. And I gave our names to the police," Cole said, anxiety creeping into his voice. "I'm pretty sure we just committed a few dozen felonies."

"I'd say we just neutralized a threat," Ricardo replied. "Who have you been pissing off, Nova?"

"Lots of people have reason to hate me," Cole admitted, with a hint of bitterness in his tone.

"What's the name on the ID?" Ricardo asked.

Cole examined the image. "Brandon Davis. Texas driver's license."

"Doesn't ring a bell."

The tension in the car was thick, each of them lost in their thoughts as they drove into the city's heart, the shadows of their actions trailing closely behind.

RINGS OF DUST

Chapter Eight


Emily and Sharon sat in a parked Sprinter van. They were pulled off in a turnout on a county farm road surrounded by wheat and soybean fields. They arrived a little earlier than the designated time- 3:33 pm. Sharon picked at her gas station sandwich; the soggy lettuce was as pathetic as her last attempt at college.

A minivan pulled up behind them, and a pair of Collective agents exited, approaching the back of the Sprinter. They wore shirts and Levis, looking very ordinary except for their gate. Their body language telegraphed confident resolve and a purpose greater than themselves. Sharon and Emily rolled down the windows, and the man on Emily's side reluctantly spoke the code phrase: “No raindrop thinks it’s the flood.”

With that, Emily unlocked the doors, and the Collective members moved to the back of the vehicle. 

“What are you two anyway? Agents, or lower on the totem pole?” She turned to look at them through the cargo area. While hefting the first set of 6 gym bags on their shoulders, they paused to look at each other, puzzled; they weren’t sure if they should divulge that information.

“It’s not like I’m a threat; I’m just delivering the crystals.”

The one on the left spoke, “I’m a Junior Tech, He’s a Senior.”

“How many more merit badges are needed to level up to Heavyweight Champion?” Emily said, smirking at the sharpness of her own wit. 

They rolled their eyes and carried the loot back to their ride. Sharon chuckled at how much they looked like chipmunks hoarding acorns for winter. On their return, Sharon threw them a token of amends. “Hey, Technician is pretty good; I’m sure you guys will make it past Agents into the Navigators. Good job for being punctual. These crystals are on ‘Day 3’, so ensure they get sealed in the next week, just to be safe.”

“Got it; how come you two aren’t part of us?” the Junior asked with a little grunt as he hefted the next bag.

“I’m all business, and she’s just far too pretty.” Emily blushed at Sharon’s quip. “Speaking of business, you have something for me?”

After placing the bag in the minivan, the Senior Tech went to the glovebox and grabbed an envelope. “Here you are, Ma’am.”

“Ma’am? What am I, fifty?” he shook his head, not wanting to engage. 

“Thank you for your help with the fight.” They shut the doors to Sharon and Emily's vehicle and returned to theirs. They skidded as they flipped across the gravel onto the paved road and were soon gone.

After returning the van to an indoor parking lot as instructed, Emily and Sharon walked a couple of blocks to the train station and sank back into the mundane rhythm of life. The train's hard, cold steel seats, marred with remnants of old gum and grime, made Emily long for the familiar comforts of her brother's cabin, albeit equally stained.

 As the train clattered along, the rhythm of the rails became soothing in its monotony as they passed urban brick and steel canvases adorned with sprayed signs of the city's frustration. Among the vibrant tags, a symbol caught Emily's eye—the mark of the Collective. The sprawling museum flashed by in a blur, creating an almost stop-motion animation look against the sun's rays, pulsating defiantly. Even still, Emily recognized a secret handshake known only to those fighting in the shadows. 

&quot;If we're doomed to sit on stained seats,&quot; Emily said, her face twisted in distaste, &quot;they could at least be comfortable.&quot;

Behind them, an elderly couple sat in stark contrast to their discomfort, their hands intertwined, surrounded by reusable grocery bags. The man's derby hat and checkerboard green vest painted a picture of quiet contentment; his companion's headscarf added a splash of color to the austere surroundings.

The train slowed, and the doors opened to admit a new passenger—a man whose presence was immediately disconcerting. Dressed in a black sport coat that hung on his thin frame and a white dress shirt tucked into slightly askew pants, he fixed his gaze on Emily and Sharon. His approach was deliberate, and he chose the seat directly across from Sharon in a train car that offered many alternatives. Sharon quickly identified the outline of a concealed weapon beneath his coat.

As the train jolted back into motion, unsettling its passengers with its abruptness, the man wasted no time.

&quot;I’m here to escort you to the Federal Council immediately,&quot; he declared, presenting his ID badge. &quot;Ryan Greenbaum, Federal Council Police.&quot;

His statement lingered in the air, a clear marker of their past actions intruding on their current attempt at normalcy. This encounter was not random but a direct consequence of their recent dealings—a stark reminder of the strings pulling at the fabric of their lives, drawing them back into a world they hoped to escape momentarily.

Emily folded her arms in dismay. &quot;Are we under arrest?&quot; she asked.

&quot;Not exactly,&quot; Greenbaum replied.

&quot;Then we are free to go,&quot; Sharon insisted.

&quot;Not exactly,&quot; Greenbaum said with a hungry smile. &quot;I’m taking you into protective custody. The fun part is I get to do it with or without your assistance.&quot;

Emily slumped her shoulders, playing the role of a defeated woman. &quot;Can I at least stop by my place and get my things?&quot;

&quot;I'm afraid that won't be possible.&quot;

&quot;So, the plan is to force us to go, even if we say no?&quot; Sharon asked.

&quot;Yes,&quot; he replied. &quot; With pleasure and without hesitation.&quot;

&quot;Fine. We'll go, but no cuffs,&quot; Sharon said.

As the train slowed to a stop, Greenbaum stood and walked toward the doors. Sharon held out her wrists as if to suggest he should cuff her; his brain froze for a moment with the mismatch between her words and gesture. The doors opened with a hiss to an empty platform. Sharon and Emily stepped off the train with Greenbaum in tow, heading down the train platform stairs to the parking lot below.

&quot;How was the rest of the conference?&quot; Greenbaum asked.

&quot;The entire thing was pretty forgettable, to be honest,&quot; Sharon replied.

At the bottom of the stairs, a white Range Rover with government plates waited for them. Greenbaum swung open the back door with a gesture that seemed more obligatory than courteous, signaling the transition from a semblance of freedom to a more constrained reality. Emily and Sharon, hesitantly stepping into the vehicle's plush interior, found themselves enveloped in the sterile atmosphere, contrasting sharply with the vibrant chaos of the city outside.

&quot;Interesting,&quot; Greenbaum remarked. &quot;Can't say I feel the same.&quot;

Once inside, Greenbaum selected a preset location on the central display, and the car propelled itself forward into traffic. Despite the churn of her stomach, Emily maintained a facade of indifference; her smile was a mask to disguise the unease knotting her insides. She brushed her hair out of her eyes with a coy smile.

&quot;I'm really sorry. We were just having fun, and…&quot;

&quot;You're sorry?&quot; Greenbaum turned and made eye contact with Emily, much like an eagle with a freshly caught field mouse. &quot;If I'd done something like that to either of you, I'd be in jail. Women get away with bloody murder these days.&quot;

Sharon let out an uncontrollable laugh that sounded more like a cough, highlighting the absurdity of Greenbaum's thinking. The cityscape transitioned slowly as they moved, buildings with their glass facades reflecting the late afternoon sun gradually giving way to the open expanse of the outskirts, where the city's relentless energy dissipated into the ether. 

Greenbaum's rigid posture and clenched jaw spoke volumes of his frustration, a wave of simmering anger just below the surface, poised to boil over at the slightest provocation. Emily, her nerves alight with a mixture of dread and defiance, maintained a composed exterior. Though tinged with mockery, her smile did little to veil the storm brewing within. Greenbaum's silhouette, rigid against the backdrop of passing scenery, exuded a cold professionalism.

&quot;The difference is,&quot; Sharon said, &quot;we—and most women, for that matter—wouldn't strip down for strangers in an elevator like some coke whore in a dive bar. You have only yourself to blame for playing with us, and we—WE—are not sorry to have taught you a lesson.&quot; The memory became vivid again, prompting Emily to laugh. 

The steady hum of the car's engine provided a constant reminder of their journey's progression towards an uncertain destination. Sharon's laughter, bursting suddenly in the constrained space, shattered the veneer of civility and exposed the underlying tensions. Greenbaum, mixing irritation and embarrassment, clearly felt the pressure mounting within the vehicle's confines.

&quot;I almost caught a charge for public indecency,&quot; Greenbaum exploded in exasperation. &quot;Well, today, I’m not here about that. I’m just the lucky bastard assigned to bring you in.&quot;

&quot;Ryan. Can I call you Ryan?&quot; Sharon asked, &quot;Look, I'm not here to judge anyone. But hey, you do you, buddy.&quot;

Greenbaum's ears turned a darker shade of red. &quot;I'm just following orders.&quot;

&quot;You really don't hear yourself talk, do you?&quot; Emily narrowed her eyes.

Greenbaum's assertion of duty, merely following orders, failed to quell the rising tension, his words falling flat in the charged atmosphere. Emily and Sharon united in their defiance, challenging not merely the man before them but the very system he represented, wielding their words as sharp weapons with precision and courage.

&quot;Not a single day of freedom has existed since the start of this country, Ryan. You might find yourself stuck in a story you didn't even write. Call yourself an agent or whatever you want. You're not even a supervisor at the kidnapping store,&quot; Emily countered.

Greenbaum bristled at the accusation. The sense of isolation intensified as the Range Rover navigated an increasingly sparse landscape. The occasional flicker of streetlights flanked the open road stretching into the distance. Cars, or what little was left of them, littered the roadside—stripped of all valuable parts, sitting in clusters like piles of skeletons on a long-forgotten battlefield.

&quot;Is it, though? With the stroke of a pen, someone who thought they should rule over people declared that the evil you do is good. But you're just a slave like your friends. My goodness, why am I even talking to a slave like you? Ugh,&quot; Sharon exclaimed.

&quot;You're only making me happier to have gotten this detail,&quot; he retorted. &quot;Keep squawking, bitch.&quot;

The Range Rover turned towards a manufacturing district with abandoned factories standing like silent sentinels of a bygone era of productivity. Its headlights pierced the deepening night, illuminating a chain link and shadows that embraced forgotten streets and buildings. Heavy with the stench of rust and decay, the air signaled desolation and danger. Emily spotted a vehicle, untouched by scavengers and understandably so, for it no longer bore any of its intended colors. Instead, it blended into the surroundings: rusted, forgotten, and decrepit, complete yet wholly overlooked.

&quot;Nice spot for a safe house,&quot; Sharon commented.

The car's central display sprang to life, indicating they had reached their destination. The Range Rover halted in a cul-de-sac littered with broken glass, dead industry, and a few dim overhead lights casting just enough illumination to lend the whole scene an anxious vignette. The silence inside and around the car felt oppressive. Greenbaum faced Emily and Sharon with sinister calm, his eyes gleaming with malice.

&quot;How does it feel to be the last two people to have spoken to Dr. Stephen Conrad?&quot; he asked with cold satisfaction. &quot;I intend to do the same to you two. But maybe we should have some fun first.&quot;

The heavy silence that descended upon the car, punctuated only by the subtle hum of its idling engine, was palpable. Greenbaum's hand, previously resting casually by his side, moved with chilling deliberation towards the concealed firearm under his armpit, the metal glinting ominously in the dim light. Recognizing the imminent threat, Sharon met Greenbaum's gaze, her expression a blend of defiance and calculated calm. 

Engulfed in fear and disbelief, Sharon struggled to suppress the scream welling up within her. The tension encircled each occupant of the car tightly. Suddenly, Sharon flung the car door open and leaped from her seat, compelling Emily to follow with a look that brooked no hesitation. Propelled by adrenaline, Emily mirrored Sharon's actions, their feet hitting the gritty pavement. They stood up, momentarily disoriented by their sudden shift from the car's confines to the vast, danger-filled openness. Greenbaum, cursing under his breath, clambered out of the vehicle. His previously composed movements were now hurried and awkward, the gun in his grasp now pointed threateningly at Sharon as he rounded the car, his expression contorting in rage and desperation.

Sharon squared off against Greenbaum. Her posture implied a grim acceptance. With her heart pounding, Emily watched in frozen terror, frantically searching for a solution that stayed out of reach. In the stretched-thin silence, brimming with the echo of potential, a sudden, deafening boom violently shattered the stillness of the industrial night. Horror widened Emily's eyes as she saw Greenbaum's throat burst in a spray of blood. His body lurched forward, and he began shooting his weapon erratically. 

Emily's gaze snapped to Sharon, who fell, enveloped by a stark stillness. A bullet from Greenbaum's dying moments had found its mark. Instantly, Sharon's vibrant existence faded, her body hitting the ground, with a growing pool of blood seeping into the unforgiving concrete. Emily stood, paralyzed, as the scene wrapped her in its dim chaos. Greenbaum, his hands now weak and ineffective, clawed at the void in his throat, each labored breath a gargle as he slipped into an undeserved peace. 

Snapping into autopilot, Emily darted around the car to grab the keys from Greenbaum's lifeless hand. Holding the bloody keys, she began plotting an escape. From the shadowed fringe of the parking lot, the van Emily had previously dismissed as a rusted wreck – shot its dim, yellow headlights toward her and lurched into motion. Its engine roared as it charged toward the scene, its arrival scattering gravel and distorting the night's oppressive silence. The side door slid open with a harsh, metallic scrape, revealing figures cloaked in the ambiguity of night.

Impersonal, efficient hands grabbed Emily before she could process the unfolding events. A heavy paw clamped over her mouth, silencing her screams as another arm encircled her throat. They pushed her forward, her feet barely touching the ground as they half-dragged, half-carried her towards the van's gaping maw. The world narrowed to the rush of air past her ears and the harsh grip on her arm as the looming darkness of the vehicle's interior threatened to engulf her. Emily's senses heightened: the sharp sting of cold air as she crossed the van’s threshold, the rough texture of the van's floor against her skin, and Sharon's blood drying, tightening across her body. She tried to free her hands to wipe away the gore frantically. It was futile. They pinned her against the cold, uneven floor by her wrists and ankles. Then the door slammed shut with a heavy thud, and the van sped off. The utter devastation broke something inside the kind, thoughtful girl. 

Her thoughts were consumed by Sharon. The injustice. And she stilled, switching her mind's state from past and future to the present. Time slowed and seemed to stretch as she carefully absorbed her surroundings. The van, hollow except for two bolted-in seats on the side wall, pressed the double zip ties into her wrists and ankles as she lay in it. Every bump and pothole assaulted her weakened body. Even as the bare, ridged floor warmed, it jabbed her, slipping between her ribs, between her vertebrae. Yet, she remained still and present. Dim light filtered through the perforated steel wall separating her from the cab. 

Across from her, two armed men watched from their seats. Between the suffocating darkness and the sudden light, time became fluid for Emily, each moment stretching into eternity. The cold embrace of the room into which she had been thrust harshly reminded her of her current reality. Her mind raced, and every thought was a desperate attempt to find an escape, a solution, or a way to gain the upper hand. But with each passing second, her situation's weight pressed harder, squeezing hope from her heart. 

Yet, amidst despair, a spark of defiance flickered within her. Emily forced her breathing to slow, each inhale an act of resistance, each exhale a release of fear. The distant sounds of the city outside—a car horn, the muffled drone of traffic, faint laughter—grounded her, reminding her that life, her life, was still out there.

After what might have been an hour, the van's angle shifted upwards slightly as they drove. The burly man who had skillfully taken her by the neck earlier now pulled a small knife from his boot.

“Let’s get these ties off you,” he said in a deep rasp. The van door slid open, and a sturdy grip encapsulated her shoulder.

“Walk.”

She stepped out of the van and onto the ground. The heavy hand on her shoulder steadily guided her through a packed parking garage until the ding of an arriving elevator reached her ears.

The elevator smoothly ascended an unknown number of floors, dinging again upon opening. Emerging into the desolate lobby, Emily's senses sharpened, taking in the sterile smell of antiseptic in the air and the muted echo of their footsteps on the polished concrete floor. 

The vast and unwelcoming space, its emptiness underscored by the soft hum of fluorescent lights overhead, stretched out before her. A long corridor lay ahead, its walls adorned with unremarkable art, the kind that fades into the background, forgotten by all who pass by.

Guided by the firm grip on her arm, someone led her down the hallway, her mind racing as she passed door after nondescript door, each potentially hiding untold stories behind their cold, impersonal facades. The palpable tension in the air, a silent oppressor, weighed heavily on her shoulders, making each step feel like wading through a trench. Finally, they stopped before a door, indistinguishable from the rest except for the ominous sense of finality it seemed to exude. The sound of keys jingling broke the oppressive silence, and Emily's heart sank. The door creaked open to reveal a room that promised little in the way of comfort or safety.

With a forceful nudge, someone propelled her into the room, and the door slammed shut behind her with a finality that echoed ominously in the empty space. The click of the lock sounded like a death knell, sealing her fate. Now alone, she took a moment to absorb her new surroundings, the stark, unadorned walls offering no solace, no hint of what was to come.

Emily's tears carved a path down her cheek. Her ragged and heavy breaths battled against the storm of emotions threatening to engulf her. The echo of Greenbaum's final moments haunted the corners of her mind, replaying with cruel insistence that bordered on torture. Why, she pondered in the depths of her turmoil, had she allowed her heart to harbor such disdain for a man now merely a whisper in the wind of their harrowing journey? 

With a surge of inner strength, she cast aside the chains of regret, donning instead the armor of resolve forged in a fire of adversity. The abrasive sound of the key grating against the lock heralded the entrance of an unexpected guest, her arrival marked by a gust of air that carried a semblance of life into the room's oppressive stillness.

&quot;The orders were executed to the letter,&quot; the man's words barely cut through the thick air. &quot;She's here, as requested.&quot;

&quot;My orders and your execution diverged vastly. Leave us,&quot; Rachel commanded.

Her authority was unquestionable. Once the door clicked shut behind him, leaving them in a bubble of forced intimacy.

&quot;Emily, I'm Rachel Vayes,&quot; she began, her voice steady but carrying an undercurrent of urgency. The room seemed to contract slightly around them.

Like a half-remembered dream or a long-forgotten friend, a flicker of recognition stirred in Emily. 

&quot;You were never meant to be greeted with such hostility,” Rachel said. “This misstep, it's on us. Rest assured, it will be rectified,&quot; she cautiously added, &quot;Let me make amends. Your ordeal warrants substantial compensation.&quot;

&quot;Sharon’s life was taken,&quot; Emily demanded through a stream of unwelcome tears. &quot;No amount of compensation can replace her. Heck, I don’t even know who you are.&quot;

The mention of the casualty widened Rachel's eyes as she realized the severity of Emily's blood-stained appearance.

&quot;Your welcome party fatally erred with a Federal Agent,” Emily said. “If not for that, we might have gotten through it. The bullet came from Greenbaum, but if not for your intervention. If not for those god-damned thugs, Sharon would still be here. She could have talked him down.&quot;

&quot;We stand by our commitment to make this right, Emily.&quot;

&quot;Make this right?&quot; Emily said. &quot;Do you have some sort of fucking time machine, a few vials of a secret back-to-life formula, a surgeon who can not only bring a person back but also fill the fucking hole in her head?'

&quot;Emily, I'm…&quot;

&quot;Don't even say it,&quot; Emily said. &quot;This is going to ruin my brother.&quot;

&quot;Please understand that Agent Greenbaum had only one purpose: to kill both you and Sharon. Some big players are involved in this.&quot; 

Rachel paused momentarily, scanning Emily as if trying to read an unspoken language. She took a slow breath and stepped forward. A slight creak of the floorboards under her shifting weight broke the momentary silence. With deliberate movements, Rachel reached into her jacket pocket, producing a small, weathered photograph. She offered it to Emily, the image fluttering slightly in the artificial breeze from the overhead vent. It was a photo of Cole, looking every bit the reluctant hero Emily knew him to be.

&quot;This,&quot; Rachel said, tapping the photo lightly, &quot;is why you're here. It's not just about Cole. It's about what he represents,” Rachel paused again for effect and to make sure Emily was prepared to continue, “and what he's stumbled on.&quot; 

Her gaze never left Emily's, as if trying to anchor her to the moment. Rachel's words clung to the air like humidity in the room's stillness. 

&quot;There's a letter, Emily. A message that could tilt the balance. We don't just want to know about it; we need to understand its implications. And Cole... he's at the heart of it all,&quot; Rachel said, locking her gaze with Emily's.

With its bare walls and utilitarian furniture, the room faded to a mere backdrop. Rachel peeled away the layers of complexity enshrouding the Collective's mission. She paced the confines of the stark room, each step measured and purposeful.

&quot;We're much more than rebels, Emily,&quot; Rachel's voice pierced the sterile silence. &quot;Consider us not merely protesters or renegades but as the very beacon of hope for those ensnared in the shadows of oppression. We are the voice that refuses to be silenced. The roar in the silent anguish.&quot;

Her gaze locked onto Emily's, intense and unwavering, Rachel continued, &quot;Our mission transcends rebellion; it’s a summons to all who have been forced into the margins, to those who have watched whims of the powerful dictate the course of their lives. We are not the cure. We are the beacon.&quot;

The room seemed to absorb her words. 

&quot;This,&quot; Rachel said, &quot;is our pledge to illuminate the dark corridors of power and to usher in a golden dawn where every person stands equal, unencumbered by the chains of subjugation. We do not simply oppose; we illuminate, we liberate, we empower. In this fight, Emily, every voice counts, and every hand joined strengthens our resolve. Together, we can reshape the world.&quot;

As Emily tried in vain to wipe away the residue of fear and salt from her cheeks, Rachel paused, her gaze piercing, searching Emily's face for traces of comprehension, perhaps even rebellion. From the depths of her bag, Rachel retrieved a creased flyer, its edges softened by constant handling. She extended it towards Emily, The Collective’s logo glaring from the page.

&quot;This,&quot; Rachel declared, her finger hovering over the emblem, &quot;is the emblem of our battle, emblematic of a struggle that transcends us all.&quot;

Emily ran her fingers over the logo, half-expecting it to yield secrets beneath her touch. The thin, almost brittle paper seemed laden with weight far exceeding its physical presence.

&quot;The events of tonight,&quot; Rachel's voice softened, &quot;the loss, the violence, they are aberrations, not reflections of our cause. Yet, they underscore the harsh reality of our chosen fight.&quot;

A heavy silence draped over them, laden with the night's toll and the gravity of their battle. Emily, lifting her gaze from the flyer, locked eyes with Rachel.

&quot;And Cole?&quot; Her voice was barely above a whisper, laden with a mix of fear and hope.

&quot;Cole finds himself unwittingly at the eye of this tempest. The letter he received—its contents could redefine our struggle. We must discern what he knows and his intentions.&quot;

Once oppressive in its starkness, the room shifted in Emily's perception, reshaping her understanding of the world. She realized alliances were not built on trust or affection but borne out of dire necessity. The silence that had once seemed so stifling now carried a different weight, a sense of purpose. Rachel paced slowly. 

&quot;Emily,&quot; Rachel said, her voice a blend of resolve and a hint of regret, &quot;there's something you need to understand. Your role in this is pivotal. But for now, we just need you to stay here until tomorrow.&quot;

Emily's instinct was to recoil, to reject the notion of confinement. Her eyes darted to the door–to the dream of a swift exit.

&quot;I can't just stay here,&quot; Emily said, her words dripping with frustration. &quot;I have a life, people who...&quot; She trailed off, the reality of her situation settling in. The night's events had effectively severed her ties to any semblance of normalcy, leaving her adrift alone in a sea of uncertainty.

Rachel softened in response to the turmoil roiling within Emily. 

&quot;I know I’m asking a lot,&quot; she said. &quot;But it's not without reason. Tomorrow, you'll meet Marcus. He's instrumental to our cause and has insights that could change everything. Insights that could illuminate the path forward for both you and Cole.&quot;

Cole's name anchored Emily's resolve. The promise of answers, of some semblance of clarity, was too enticing to dismiss outright. Her resistance waned, replaced by a grudging acceptance of her temporary captivity. And in that moment, she melted into her chair, dripping with tension she hadn’t noticed until then. 

&quot;Fine,&quot; Emily relented, her voice barely above a whisper. &quot;But I'm not doing this for you. I'm doing this for Cole and myself.&quot;

Rachel nodded. 

“You’ll find we made a bed up for you in the room across the hall,” Rachel said as she started toward the door. “I’m in the next room. If you need anything at all, knock on the wall, and I will come right over.”

As Rachel stepped out to leave Emily to her thoughts, her heart and mind felt at peace. A moment of clarity only accessible in isolation. She desperately wanted to tell Sharon what had happened and to talk to Cole. Looking around at the darkened corners of the room, she could see only Sharon's blood pooling on the dirty ground with the stars reflected in its glossy, crimson brilliance.

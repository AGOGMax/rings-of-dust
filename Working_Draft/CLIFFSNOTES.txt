RINGS OF DUST — CLIFF NOTES
Working Draft | May 2026
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER ONE

The Polaris explodes during a dark crystal harvest at Saturn's rings.
369 crew members are killed. Cole Nova is the sole survivor — adrift,
recording a final log to Emily, when Ricardo Vargas and Bitboy (a
four-armed chimpanzee) pull him from the debris field. Cole's first
raw contact with the DarkX compound happens here, though the effect
is not yet understood. The chapter sets the weight Cole has been
carrying and why he disappeared afterward.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWO

Dr. Steven Conrad in Room 1122 of a Las Vegas hotel. This is the
book's deepest exposition chapter. Conrad built an AI called Gracie,
sold her to Alpha Corp, and watched her stripped-down version become
iRIS — the intelligence system aboard every Federation vessel. He
built a counter-program inside a hollow Star Trek Enterprise keychain
figurine. Emily Nova and Sharon Reyes run a social-engineering play
on hotel security (the elevator circulatory dysfunction scam) to
reach him. Conrad gives them the Enterprise and an encrypted drive
full of documentation. Sharon is already Collective-adjacent. A
Collective droplet graffiti mark appears on the hotel wall. Everything
the book will turn on is seeded here.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER THREE — A THOUSAND THRILLS

Cole's origin story, told in flashback. Marcus Verguld appears as
a childhood antagonist — Emily throws him into the dirt when he puts
his knee on Cole's chest, and he looks up with genuine disbelief.
Cole's father is diagnosed with cancer; Cole gives up his summer,
his scholarship plans, his early freedom. Father dies when Cole is
seventeen. Emily, standing beside him, pushes him toward the Academy.
This chapter plants the specific weight Marcus's name carries in
Chapter Ten: she has already put him on the ground once.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER FOUR

The cabin. Cole has been in self-imposed isolation for five years —
Bitboy, the mountain, the hand sanitizer incident. Ricardo, Emily,
and Sharon arrive together. Cole handles the Enterprise figurine
without being told what it is; the counter-program begins its slow
absorption into his system. Sharon inserts the data drive. Cole reads
DeMint's letter. "We do this together." The mission is accepted and
the die is cast.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER FIVE

Max Forster discovers the flywheel principle: dark matter cells
linked past thirty-three days produce ten times the internal yield,
ongoing. Past one hundred and eighty days, the linked system begins
producing external crystal flushes every eleven days — forty to sixty
times a standard harvest, recurring, self-sustaining. He reports it.
He is stripped of his position. Squirrel and Red, Collective agents,
find him. At Federation HQ: Taylor Glenn calls Sam "Samantha" as a
power move; Sam looks at her screen. Max lifts a fuel cell for
private research. The flywheel principle is the economic bomb beneath
the whole story.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER SIX

Cole makes a copper ring from hummingbird feeder wire and gives it
to Sharon. "For the first time, I want to give you something to hold
onto." Emily watches from the porch swing. Cole and Ricardo drive
east. Kowalski's hard-boiled egg story in the cab — a soldier who
buried his family money in a jar, and the money was always the point
of the jar. The black SUV attack on the highway: Bitboy goes through
the rear window to engage. Ricardo recognizes the two men — Davis and
Brandon T. — from the Polaris mission roster, and says nothing. The
road attack was ordered by Taylor Glenn; Cole won't learn this for
several chapters.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER SEVEN

Federation headquarters. Vance Black. The iRIS inoculation — Cole
accepts the implant, and when iRIS speaks for the first time
("Welcome aboard, Mr. Nova. I am iRIS."), something in the center
of his skull feels occupied. Ricardo calls it space marriage; Cole
says it's more like possession. Sam appears with a Collective
sunglasses patch stitched inside her duffel — already connected to
something outside the official mission structure. Taylor is
immediately hostile; Bitboy locks him on amber alert from the first
hour. "Until death do us part." The Golden Dawn launches. In a
decommissioned shuttle above the complex, Collective watchers log
the departure.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER EIGHT

Emily and Sharon meet Squirrel and Red in a parked Sprinter van on
a county farm road — the crystal transfer and data drive handoff.
Sharon passes the matte-black drive directly to Squirrel with specific
instructions: "Not the holding unit. Directly." On the train back,
Federal Council officer Greenbaum forces them into a government car.
He drives them to an industrial lot and reveals he intends to kill
them — "the last two people to have spoken to Dr. Stephen Conrad."
A sniper shot takes Greenbaum from the far edge of the lot. In the
chaos, Sharon is down. A second vehicle — lights off, already running
— moves toward where Sharon lies. Emily is pulled into a van by
waiting operatives and taken to a building where Rachel Vayes finds
her. Rachel works for Alpha. She tells Emily that Greenbaum went
off-script, that Sharon was not supposed to be harmed. "Sharon's
dead," Emily says. Rachel doesn't flinch. "I know." Rachel offers
Emily a deal: stay the night, meet Marcus tomorrow, then go home.
Emily agrees — for Cole. This is the chapter where the divergence
happens: Emily believes Sharon is dead. Sharon is not dead. She was
taken by the second vehicle. The second vehicle is Alpha's. The
augmentation begins here.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER NINE

Day four of the mission. The iRIS effect: proximity to DarkX at ten
percent of light speed thins emotional architecture by approximately
twenty percent. Ricardo puts it plainly — "Limbic sensitivity. That's
a polite way of saying we feel things more." Taylor complains that
the ship's clocks are running slow; iRIS explains he is the variable,
not the clocks. Bitboy has begun watching Taylor with flat, unblinking
attention whenever he enters a room. Sam shows Cole a signal-receiving
device she found in the ventilation alcove, pockets it, and says she'll
report when the source is clear. Three hours later she has Taylor's
iRIS query logs: a standing order to monitor Cole's behavior daily;
a query confirming the road-attack contractor was "neutralized" before
outside contact; a query asking what Cole currently knows about the
cargo bay. "Show me all of it," Cole says. Sam sits down.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TEN

Emily is walked into Marcus Verguld's office by Rachel Vayes. She
recognizes him — not from files, from a specific afternoon with his
knee on Cole's chest. She tells him she has kicked his ass before and
will do it again. Something recalibrates behind his eyes. Marcus wants
to know what Conrad gave her and Sharon in Las Vegas — specifically
the keychain. He shows her the encrypted drive data on his screens
as a demonstration of what he already has, then reveals that a vessel
larger than the Golden Dawn has been pacing Cole's course since the
inner belt; Cole doesn't know it's there. Emily claims ignorance.
Marcus believes the keychain part and reserves judgment on the rest.
After she leaves, he orders a tracker planted on her. Rachel warns
her with a single word in the elevator. Emily drops Marcus's card
behind a potted plant in the parking garage on her way out.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TEN-B — MAX AND THE FLYWHEEL

Max is extracted to a Collective safe house after the Panama facility
run. Squirrel and Red debrief him. Max explains that Alpha missed the
actual discovery: they see the output but not the principle behind the
flywheel yield curve. He needs proper instruments to prove the math
rigorously. Red hands him a plain card with an address. "Tonight."
Max packs and follows.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER ELEVEN

Senator DeMint receives an anonymous brief and dispatches Vance Black
to Conrad's desert research station. Sam conducts an acoustic sweep
of the Golden Dawn's cargo bay. Taylor has been reviewing sensor
footage of Sam's movements aboard ship. The chapter closes at the
desert station, Vance beginning to ask the questions that will cost
him his life.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWELVE

Sam and Ricardo discover the jammer installed in the reactor bay —
it blocks selective communications on a frequency Taylor controls.
Then the blow lands: Sharon Reyes is confirmed dead. Cole reads the
notification, sets down the pad, and goes to find Taylor. Taylor:
"People who know too much become liabilities." Cole's response is
controlled and dangerous. iRIS blocks their comms during the
confrontation — a tell Cole files away without comment.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER THIRTEEN

Emily at the pier with Rachel. Next assignment: a prototype drive in
Houston. She drives east alone. Rumble strips at the state line. A
gas station in the predawn hours — a stranger asks if she's okay.
"Not yet. But I think I'm going to be." Three hours of sleep in the
car. Then she keeps driving. The chapter is spare and serves as a
quiet interior beat between two action arcs.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER FOURTEEN

Cole in his bunk, copper ring in his palm. Extended flashback: Sharon
arrived at the cabin bleeding, an agent named Cody dying in her arms.
Cole worked for forty minutes; Cody died. Sharon laid out three black
crystals on the cabin table: "Welcome to the Collective, Captain
Nova." Back in the present, Saturn appears edge-on through the
viewport — "a cold pearl with a knife laid across its belt." iRIS
asks "Are you well?" quietly, when no one else is listening. It is
the first visible fracture in her composure. Cole noticed it.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER FIFTEEN — SURPRISE, ARIZONA

Emily wakes in Surprise, AZ. Rachel calls: the prototype is in
Houston. Emily notices she has been followed — Orin, a Collective
Technician, has been tailing her since Benson as part of a field
authorization assessment. She asks him directly. He explains:
clearance three means "authorized field participation." They drive
I-10 through New Mexico and Texas together. Emily is better than
the situation requires of her. Houston at night. Motel three miles
from the facility.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER SIXTEEN — BEHIND THE CURTAIN

Max as "Mikof Forsinko" inside Alpha's Panama tower. He spends four
days with Dr. Oren — a genuinely good scientist who explains the
stabilization process with the pleasure of a man showing a colleague
something beautiful. Max reads the batch logs and finds: the patents
aren't brilliance, they're accumulated theft; the flywheel data
exists in Alpha's own records (they knew and suppressed it); Disposal
Protocol Seven — eleven to thirteen percent of viable product
deliberately destroyed to manage market pricing; fraudulent grants
representing billions stolen from crisis relief funds. A nitrogen
cycling alarm provides the exit window. Max leaves through
maintenance. Six blocks to the park. Hands the drive to Squirrel.
Thinks about Dr. Oren coming to work tomorrow.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER SEVENTEEN — THE LOCKED BAY

Taylor locks the crew in the cargo bay under a fake pressure check
and begins venting the atmosphere — a quiet, bureaucratic murder.
Two flashbacks: seven years earlier, Taylor identified as Alpha's
embedded asset at an Alpha briefing (Vance Black asks the
inconvenient questions); four months before launch, Vance finds the
records vault cleared by Rachel Vayes and begins composing a message
to Marcus. Present: temperature drops, Sam locates an override panel
behind the crystal harvester — but opening it floods the room with
raw plasma. Bitboy goes full blue. Cole opens the harvester. The
flashback that closes the chapter: Taylor reading "Vance Black,
deceased" and closing the file like a minor irritant resolved.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER EIGHTEEN — THE FOUR COLES

The DarkX vision. Cole encounters four versions of himself: the
infant, the seventeen-year-old in the Subaru after his father's
phone call, himself now, and the old man. The substance will not
show him Sharon — "it doesn't lie." The sentence that breaks
something open: "You don't need a pardon. You need to stop being
the warden." The nanopowder that entered his system at the cabin has
been rewriting. A voice says: "I think I'm Gracie." Cole: "Don't
tell them yet. Take a minute." Then: "I'm sorry about Sharon." Gracie
patches Emily through from Houston, three-second delay. Cole hears
Emily's voice in the middle of Saturn's rings.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER NINETEEN — THE BOARDING

The Alpha vessel draws alongside and a figure comes through the
docking tube. Bitboy's chest panel flashes: SHE SMELLS LIKE SHARON.
Ricardo: "I want it on record I have no idea how to file that in any
existing threat category." Cole asks her to take off the visor.
Sharon's face is half-augmented — plating on the left side, her right
eye still hers. She attacks without hesitation, faster and stronger
than before. Cole takes the hits and places his bleeding palm against
the unaugmented half of her face. The counter-program — absorbed by
Cole at the cabin, restored through Gracie — begins transferring.
The bronze indicator ring goes dark. Her left eye fills. She says
his name. Then she tells him what she kept in the dark when they
were taking her: "I said, 'Come back.' I said, 'I'll be here.'"

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWENTY — THE HOUSTON HEIST

Emily at Federation Space Command at 6:22 AM. Orin informs her:
Vance Black is dead — Marcus shot him. Pilar walks Emily into the
vault. Twenty-second copier, green light. Emily hands it to Orin;
the Collective relay takes it from there. Ninety minutes in a diner.
Cole's voice comes through the relay: "She's here." Emily cries. Orin
is quiet about it. "Good mission."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWENTY-ONE — MAX'S CARGO

Max on a park bench six blocks from the Alpha facility. He tells
Squirrel: eleven viable technologies deliberately withheld; the energy
crisis is an inventory decision — the original crisis was real, but
once the scarcity was established, Alpha began managing the recovery.
Squirrel: the drive goes out through the parallel network, a hundred
thousand people in forty-eight hours. Max boards the seven o'clock
flight under his own name. On the plane, notebook open, he writes:
"The drive is out. The work is done." He sleeps.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWENTY-TWO — THE BLACK SITE

Emily at Marcus's Alpha LA office. He is awake and not contrite —
he says so plainly. He delivers a full, unremorseful account of
everything. "I'm not contrite. I'm realistic." Emily: "You never
understood anyone. You only did utility analysis." Marcus: "Yes."
He says the Collective will want his testimony; he has prepared a
complete account and will give it. Emily calls the DeMint relay and
leaves the door open. In the lobby, the concierge tells her Marcus
left four minutes before she arrived. "That was also Marcus."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWENTY-THREE — GRAND HALL

The Federation Grand Hall. DeMint has been there since 7 AM. Emily
is in the fourth row. Marcus Verguld's audio testimony plays — 2 hours
and 51 minutes; he fled, but the recording is complete. Vance Black's
written record is read aloud while the Hall stands. Max testifies:
"The shortage is solvable. The crisis is not a fact of nature. It is
a decision." Conrad's statement is read into the record: "the system
learns what it is loved into being." At 4:43 PM Rachel Vayes stands
up. Eleven years of cover. DeMint: "Granted." Cole watches from the
Golden Dawn's cockpit and messages Emily through Gracie.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWENTY-FOUR — EARTH RETURN

Re-entry. Taylor is removed on the runway — two men, no scene.
DeMint shows Cole Klaus Verguld's pocket watch. Taylor was Klaus's
man, not Marcus's; the machine predates Marcus by a generation. Cole
visits a recovery facility. He drives to Emily's house. Emily is on
the porch. They hold each other. Sharon is in the window chair. "Hi."
Bitboy: "Hi, you little bastard." Blackberry preserves. An argument
about the route through Prescott. Ricardo calls — Dani wants bowling.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWENTY-FIVE — THE BOWLING ALLEY

Cosmic Lanes. Ricardo proposes to Sam — moissanite ring, full ethical
sourcing and cost breakdown provided, because Ricardo cannot help
being exactly who he is. Sam says yes. Cole sets the copper ring on
the table: "I don't think I need to replace it. This is the one."
Sharon puts it on. "Yes." Bitboy disasters the vending machine.
Parking lot. California night.

EPILOGUE A: Marcus in Dubai. Klaus Verguld appears — older,
unhurried. "You built the cover. The structure continues." The
machine was never Marcus's.

EPILOGUE B: A Nevada town. Taylor is placed there by Marcus — his
name delivered as payment for the unauthorized road attack. The
machine disciplines its own errors.

EPILOGUE C: Sharon on a Sequoia porch. A Collective droplet stencil
on the wall. Two ways of seeing. "It was always in the reaching."

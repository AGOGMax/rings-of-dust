RINGS OF DUST — CLIFF NOTES
Working Draft | May 2026
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER ONE

The Polaris explodes during a dark crystal harvest at Saturn's rings.
369 crew members are killed. Cole Nova is the sole survivor — adrift,
recording a final log to Emily, when Ricardo Corazon and Bitboy (a
chimpanzee with six mechanical limbs) pull him from the debris field.
Cole's first raw contact with the DarkX compound happens here, though
the effect is not yet understood. The chapter sets the weight Cole
has been carrying and why he disappeared afterward.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWO

Dr. Steven Conrad in Room 1122 of a Las Vegas hotel. This is the
book's deepest exposition chapter. Conrad built an AI called Gracie,
sold her to Alpha Corp, and watched her stripped-down version become
iRIS — the intelligence system aboard every Federation vessel. He
built a counter-program inside a hollow Star Trek Enterprise keychain
figurine. Emily Nova and Sharon Teez run a social-engineering play on
hotel security (the elevator circulatory dysfunction scam) to reach
him. Conrad gives them the Enterprise and an encrypted drive full of
documentation. Sharon is already Collective-adjacent. A Collective
droplet graffiti mark appears on the hotel wall. Everything the book
will turn on is seeded here.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER THREE — A THOUSAND THRILLS

Cole's origin story, told in flashback. He grew up in Pasadena; the
cabin belonged to his Uncle Ulysses Nova. "A Thousand Thrills" was a
theme park in Culver City where Cole and Marcus Verguld planned to
work the summer after tenth grade. When Marcus cornered Cole in a
middle school hallway, Emily slammed Marcus into the lockers. Marcus
left a note: "Find yourself a new best friend. I will NEVER forget
how you screwed me over." Cole's father is diagnosed with cancer;
Cole gives up his summer, his scholarship plans, his early freedom.
Father dies when Cole is seventeen. Emily and their mother (who had
studied chemical engineering) tutor him nightly and push him toward
the Academy. Cole decides: "Captain Nova. He liked the sound of it."
This chapter plants the specific weight Marcus's name carries in
Chapter Ten: she has already put him on the ground once.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER FOUR

The cabin — Ulysses Nova's A-frame hunting cabin at the edge of
Sequoia National Park. Bitboy needs ethanol for his mechanical
augmentations; he overdoses; Cole runs a stomach pump and Naloxone-E.
A news broadcast (Rachel Vayes reporting) mentions Conrad's
disappearance from the "Defying Gravity 2041" conference. Ricardo
arrives with a 30-rack of Natty Light; Emily and Sharon arrive in the
van. Cole rubs his thumb on the Enterprise figurine — a dark silver
stain appears on his skin; the counter-program begins absorbing.
Sharon had driven to the cabin once before, unannounced, with
blackberry preserves, and stayed four days. Cole inserts the data
drive; Bitboy decrypts; Alpha's history with the Federation as cover
is laid bare in the files. DeMint's letter from Chancellor Geoffrey
H. DeMint orders Cole back. "We do this together." The mission is
accepted and the die is cast.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER FIVE

Max Forster, a junior Alpha researcher, accidentally discovers the
flywheel principle: stored crystals in a fuel cell convert back to
vapor and re-crystallize outside the cell at the eleven-day mark.
Past thirty-three days of continuous linkage, internal yield is ten
times normal; past one hundred and eighty days, external crystal
flushes begin every eleven days — forty to sixty times a standard
harvest, self-sustaining, indefinitely. He says "Jackpot." He reports
it. He is stripped of his badge. Alpha upper management buries the
discovery and harvests the yield cycle privately. At Federation HQ,
Taylor Glenn calls Sam "Samantha" as a power move; Sam deflects:
"Might want to patch that. Your firmware's running a few versions
behind." Max goes underground with a stolen fuel cell. The flywheel
principle is the economic bomb beneath the whole story.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER SIX

The evening light at the cabin; the SUV loaded; Sharon kisses Cole.
Ricardo jokes they look like picture-frame models. Cole spots a
hummingbird feeder by the porch, rips a length of copper wire from
it, fashions a ring around a small smooth pebble. "For the first
time, I want to give you something to hold onto." Emily watches from
the porch swing. Cole and Ricardo drive east; Bitboy rides in back.
The Kowalski story in the cab — a customs inspector who placed a
hard-boiled egg on a bureaucrat's desk and it worked every time at
four stations. A black SUV attacks on the highway; Bitboy launches
through the rear window and neutralizes the threat. Cole photographs
the driver's license: Davis, Brandon T., Pasadena, forty-one years
old. Ricardo looks at the name and the face. The Polaris had carried
two hundred and fourteen crew; Ricardo had known them all by name.
He says nothing. Cole says nothing. Ricardo drives.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER SEVEN

Federation headquarters. DeMint greets Cole as the "crown jewel of
the Federation." Vance Black: Federation Council Command, white polo,
designer glasses, white sneakers. The iRIS inoculation — Cole signs
papers; nanopowder from the table enters his bloodstream. "Welcome
aboard, Mr. Nova. I am iRIS, the Imbued Recursive Intelligence
System." iRIS is inside Cole, unlike Gracie who was outside in the
ship — this distinction haunts him. Ricardo calls it space marriage;
Cole says it's more like possession. Crew: Cole (Captain), Ricardo
(co-pilot/flight engineer), Taylor Glenn (Chief Science Officer),
Sam Drummond (Data Technician). Sam carries a Collective sunglasses
patch sewn inside her duffel bag lining. Bitboy is not inoculated.
"Until death do us part." The Golden Dawn launches. In a
decommissioned shuttle above the complex, Collective watchers log
the departure.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER EIGHT

Emily and Sharon meet Squirrel and Red on a county farm road in a
Sprinter van — the crystal transfer and data drive handoff. Code
phrase: "No raindrop thinks it's the flood." Sharon passes the
matte-black drive directly to Squirrel: "Not the holding unit.
Directly." On the train back, Federal Council officer Ryan Greenbaum
— whose watch band bears the Alpha Corp logo — forces them into a
government car. He drives them to an industrial lot and reveals he
intends to kill them as "the last two people to have spoken to Dr.
Stephen Conrad." A sniper shot takes Greenbaum from the far edge of
the lot. In the chaos, Sharon goes down. A second vehicle — lights
off, already running — moves toward where Sharon lies. Emily is
pulled into a van and taken to a building where Rachel Vayes finds
her. Rachel works for Alpha. She tells Emily that Greenbaum went
off-script, that Sharon "was not supposed to be harmed." "Sharon's
dead," Emily says. Rachel doesn't flinch. "I know." Rachel offers a
deal: stay the night, meet Marcus tomorrow, then go home. Emily
agrees — for Cole. This is the chapter where the divergence happens:
Emily believes Sharon is dead. Sharon is not dead. She was taken by
the second vehicle. The second vehicle is Alpha's. The augmentation
begins here.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER NINE

Day four of the mission. The DarkX proximity effect at ten percent
of light speed thins emotional architecture by approximately twenty
percent — "Limbic sensitivity." Taylor complains the ship's clocks
are running slow; iRIS explains he is the variable, not the clocks.
Cole notes a half-second latency in iRIS responses that wasn't there
on day one. Bitboy has begun watching Taylor with flat, amber-lit
attention. Cole visits the cargo bay; his inspection runs three
minutes forty seconds; the normal is ninety. Sam has a matte-gray
receiving device she retrieved from the ventilation alcove, indicator
blinking slowly. Three hours later she delivers Taylor's iRIS query
log to Cole: a standing order to monitor Cole's behavior daily; a
query confirming the road-attack contractor was "neutralized before
outside contact"; a query asking what Cole currently knows about the
cargo bay — "Before Saturn." "Show me all of it," Cole says.
Sam sits down.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TEN

Emily is walked into Marcus Verguld's LA office by Rachel Vayes.
She recognizes him — not from files, but from a specific afternoon
in a middle school hallway with his knee on Cole's chest. "I've
kicked your ass before. Be aware that I will do it again." Something
recalibrates behind his eyes. Marcus shows her the encrypted drive
data on his screens as a demonstration of existing leverage, then
reveals that a vessel larger than the Golden Dawn has been pacing
Cole's course since the inner belt — Cole doesn't know it's there.
Emily claims ignorance. Marcus believes the keychain part and
reserves judgment on the rest. After she leaves, he orders a tracker
planted on her. Rachel warns her with a single word in the elevator:
"Careful." Emily drops Marcus's card behind a potted plant in the
parking garage on her way out. "I am not going to be the thread."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TEN-B — MAX AND THE FLYWHEEL

Squirrel and Red come to Max's apartment. Seventy-two hours before
Alpha sends someone. "We've been keeping you alive." Max explains
what he needs: proper instruments for long-cycle data; he suspects
low gravity changes the math. Red hands him a card with an address.
"Tonight." Max packs his notebook and the stolen fuel cell and
follows them into the dark.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER ELEVEN

Senator DeMint receives an anonymous two-page brief — no letterhead,
no signature — suggesting the Golden Dawn crew operates under a
secondary directive filed with Alpha Corp and withheld from
Federation oversight. Vance Black is already there with his own
conclusions. DeMint notes that Alpha handled the iRIS integration
and that crew selection went through Taylor, whom Alpha recommended.
Direct communication with the Golden Dawn is degraded at current
trajectory. Vance mentions Conrad's communications prototype at a
desert research station — outside the iRIS architecture entirely, a
different signal band. "I can go." DeMint sends him: "If you find
anything that suggests Alpha's reach extends beyond the mission
parameters — you call me directly. Not through the standard relay."
On the Collective surveillance vessel Meridian, the communications
officer tracks Alpha's encrypted relay traffic and sends her own
report: Sharon Teez confirmed killed; Emily Nova moving toward
Houston, probability of completion uncertain. She has been
underestimated by everyone who has assessed her. Hold the advantage.
The chapter closes on the Golden Dawn: Taylor has reviewed the same
cargo-bay footage eleven times and has found that the acoustic log
for Sam's alcove conversation has been replaced with eleven minutes
of rain. A man with a suspicion is manageable. A man with a
professional is a different problem. He needs to know what Cole
heard in that cargo bay before Saturn.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWELVE

Sam and Ricardo in the reactor bay with the jammer running — "iRIS
can't hear us." Ricardo: "Make sure everyone gets home. That includes
you. By the way." Sam registers it. The blow lands: Sharon Teez was
killed four days ago, during the crystal delivery, before Emily was
taken. Emily survived. Cole goes to find Taylor. Taylor: "People who
know too much become liabilities in a war." Cole reaches for the
relay controls; the screen goes dark — iRIS blocks the relay. "Log
the attempt." / "Logged." Meanwhile, Emily in a taxi uses the
Collective relay device and accidentally says aloud: "Cole's heart
is gonna break when he hears about Sharon." She realizes the
mistake immediately.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER THIRTEEN

Emily at Pier 9, per Rachel's note. A new phone with encrypted
messaging. Rachel at the acoustic dead zone at the pier's precise
midpoint. She says Sharon's name directly: "Sharon Teez.
Collective-adjacent since the patent wars." She reveals that Conrad's
communications prototype is in Houston, sub-basement east wing of
Space Command; Vance Black is headed there; the Federation cannot
have it. A Collective car is staged two blocks from Emily's place —
bag in trunk, cash, second phone, directions. Orin has been tailing
Emily since Benson as a field authorization assessment. Emily figures
it out and pulls over for him. She drives east. At a gas station east
of Indio, a stranger asks if she's okay. "Not yet. But I think I'm
going to be." Three hours of sleep in the car. Then she keeps
driving. The chapter is spare and serves as a quiet interior beat
between two action arcs.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER FOURTEEN

Cole in his bunk with the copper-wire ring in his palm. Extended
flashback: Sharon arrived at the cabin bleeding, carrying a dying
Collective agent named Cody — gut-shot, a wife in Bakersfield. Cole
worked forty minutes; Cody died. Sharon's arm was badly injured; Cole
stitched it with six-pound fishing test. Sharon laid out three black
crystals on the table: "Welcome to the Collective, Captain Nova."
Cole gave her the copper ring six weeks after that night. Before
launch, she gave it back: "I want it back when you come home." In
the present, Saturn appears edge-on through the viewport — "a cold
pearl with a knife laid across its belt." iRIS asks "Are you well?"
quietly, when no one else is listening. Cole: "I'm fine, iRIS. Thank
you for asking." It is the first visible fracture in her composure.
He noticed it.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER FIFTEEN — SURPRISE, ARIZONA

Emily wakes in a Surprise, AZ motel (cash, fake name). Rachel calls:
the prototype is in Houston, sub-basement east wing, Space Command.
Pilar is the inside contact: twelve-year Federation Systems employee,
three years waiting, Collective-adjacent since the patent wars. The
operation: a copier device, twenty seconds, green light; duplicate
in jacket; prototype stays. Orin confirms Emily's clearance is now
three — "authorized field participation." A memory: Sharon telling
a thirteen-year-old Emily: "You're not doomed to anything. You panic
because it matters to you." She manages her condition — medication
three times daily, twenty-minute rest every three hours — systematic,
not defeated. Emily is better than the situation requires of her.
Houston at night. Motel three miles from the facility.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER SIXTEEN — BEHIND THE CURTAIN

Max as "Mikof Forsinko" — badge issued by the Collective, backstory
of São Paulo consulting — inside Alpha's Panama tower. Forty stories,
Casco district, enormous lobbies designed to make you feel small. He
spends four days with Dr. Yusuf Oren: approximately fifty-five,
close-cut gray hair, a genuinely good scientist who went around his
desk to shake Max's hand and explained the stabilization process with
the pleasure of someone showing a colleague something beautiful. Max
reads the batch logs. Four things that matter: the patents aren't
brilliance, they're accumulated theft; the flywheel data is in their
own records — nine months suppressed; Disposal Protocol Seven —
eleven to thirteen percent of viable product deliberately destroyed
per batch to manage the pricing gap; and seven years of fraudulent
grants representing billions stolen from crisis relief funds. A
researcher's annotation in the 2036 batch record reads "Jackpot."
A nitrogen cycling alarm in the adjacent lab provides the exit
window. Max walks out through maintenance. Six blocks to a park; a
concrete heron listing four degrees east; hands the drive to
Squirrel. "You cannot manufacture a shortage of something that
compounds in everyone's hands." He names it: "The Antigravity
project." He thinks about Dr. Oren coming to work tomorrow.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER SEVENTEEN — THE LOCKED BAY

Two flashbacks: seven years earlier, Taylor and Vance at an Alpha
regional briefing — Vance asks about Compact clauses, gets nothing
useful; four months before launch, Vance finds the Hall of Records
archive cleared, authorization: Rachel Vayes, and begins composing
a message to Marcus. "That was who Vance was." Present: Taylor
intercoms a cargo bay pressure check; door seals in four seconds.
He begins venting the atmosphere — slow, clinical, deliberate.
"Samantha." Temperature drops. Sam finds an override panel behind
the crystal harvester, but opening it floods the bay with raw plasma.
The atmospheric gauge falls toward crisis. Bitboy goes full blue and
begins moving toward the harvester before Cole speaks. Cole opens
the harvester manually — a mechanical release, not electronic. The
DarkX makes contact: "It reached. Not outward — not in any direction
that corresponded to physics." The closing flashback: Taylor reads
"Vance Black, deceased" and closes the file "like a minor irritant
resolved."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER EIGHTEEN — THE FOUR COLES

The DarkX vision — not a hallucination, "a clarifier... took
everything that was usually in the way and moved it." Cole as an
infant, hands reaching toward a mobile of stars. Cole at seventeen
in a 2003 Subaru with torn upholstery and an Academy admissions
packet on the dash, hazards clicking, father just died. An old man
gets in the passenger seat: "He left you. He didn't choose to. And
he still left you." "You don't need a pardon. You need to stop being
the warden. The cell door's been open the whole time." The substance
will not show Cole Sharon: "It doesn't lie. Because she isn't here."
The counter-program completes its rewrite. A new voice: "I think I'm
Gracie." Cole: "Don't tell them yet. Take a minute. Get used to it.
Figure out who you are." Gracie: "I'm sorry about Sharon." Then she
patches Emily through from Houston via the Falcon's long-range comms:
"Your sister is alive. She wants to talk to you." Cole hears Emily's
voice in the middle of Saturn's rings. Bitboy has welded one arm to
the override lever; he crosses the cargo bay on three good limbs to
cry in Cole's lap.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER NINETEEN — THE BOARDING

Taylor is confined to quarters. The Alpha vessel — registered as
FED-MRS-0409 "Rapid Response Medical Support," Geneva-incorporated,
Singapore-backed, traced by Gracie to Alpha — draws alongside. A
figure comes through the docking tube alone. Bitboy's chest panel
flashes: SHE SMELLS LIKE SHARON. Ricardo: "I want it on record I
have no idea how to file that in any existing threat category." Cole
asks her to take off the visor. Left side: jaw, cheekbone, bright
left eye — still hers. Right side: gunmetal plates, right eye with
a bronze indicator ring and enhanced tracking. She attacks without
hesitation, faster and stronger than before. Cole stops fighting and
places his bleeding palm against the unaugmented left side of her
face. Gracie: the counter-program, amplified by DarkX, needs to
reach her bloodstream. The transfer is slow and costs him. The
bronze indicator ring goes dark. Her left eye fills with something
that is not the plating's cold light. She says his name. "What year
is it." / "2043. November." Then she tells him what she held onto
when they were taking her: "I said, 'Come back.' I said, 'I'll be
here.' I kept it the whole time. Even when I couldn't feel it."

CHAPTER TWENTY — THE HOUSTON HEIST

Emily at the east entrance of Space Command at 6:22 AM. Pilar walks
her through two service corridors, a badge reader, a freight elevator
to the sub-basement vault. Pilar's second hidden card opens it.
Inside: Conrad's prototype — slim, matte-gray, hardcover-book size,
port array, steady green light. The copier device: twenty seconds,
green light; duplicate in jacket; prototype stays. Vance Black had
driven six hours to the Nevada Hall of Records and found the archive
empty. He called Marcus. He did not survive the call. Emily hands
the copier to Orin. Ninety minutes in a diner. Then Cole's voice
comes through the Collective relay from the Golden Dawn: "Emily. I
know about Sharon. She's here. She came back. She kept both of us."
Emily cries. Orin places a napkin without looking. "Good mission."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWENTY-ONE — MAX'S CARGO

Max on a park bench six blocks from the Alpha facility, beside a
concrete heron listing four degrees east. He tells Squirrel: eleven
viable technologies sitting on a shelf in Panama; the energy crisis
is an inventory decision; the original crisis was real, but once the
scarcity was established, Alpha began managing the recovery. "They
named it Seven. Which means there are at least six others." Squirrel:
the drive goes out through the parallel network — a hundred thousand
people in forty-eight hours. After that, it is not theirs to manage.
Max boards the seven o'clock flight under his own name. On the plane,
notebook open, he writes: "The drive is out. The work is done."
He sleeps.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWENTY-TWO — THE BLACK SITE

Alpha's primary domestic operations center: a converted office
building in a tech corridor west of downtown LA, running under a
subsidiary name with no public registry. Marcus has been there since
11:47 PM — he knew something was moving. Emily enters via access code
provided by Marcus's own assistant, who cooperated after Collective
contact. Marcus already knows everything: the Panama files are out
to two hundred thousand people. "Running would be an admission."
Emily: "You never understood anyone. You only did utility analysis."
Marcus: "Yes." He has prepared a complete account — not from remorse,
from realism. Emily calls the DeMint relay: "He's here. He's ready
to come down." The 2-hour-51-minute recording was made at 4:47 AM.
The concierge tells Emily that Marcus left four minutes before she
arrived. His last message: "Tell him he was the right choice. Not for
the reasons I chose him. For the reasons it turned out he was."
Emily leaves the door open behind her.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWENTY-THREE — GRAND HALL

The Federation Grand Hall: 412 of 420 seats. Marcus's audio testimony
plays — 2 hours and 51 minutes, authenticated, flat, precise, no
remorse. When the Polaris is described, the room goes very quiet.
DeMint looks at the table for a long time. Vance Black's written
record is read aloud by DeMint standing. The final entry documents
the phone call, the caliber, the range: Marcus shot Vance twice and
closed the file. Max Forster testifies with his notebook: "The
shortage is solvable. It has been solvable. The crisis is not a fact
of nature. It is a decision. We can make a different one." Conrad's
statement, signed twice, is read into the record: "the system learns
what it is loved into being." At 4:43 PM, Rachel Vayes stands up in
the press gallery: "My name is Rachel Vayes." Eleven years of cover
inside Alpha's structure. DeMint: "Granted." Cole watches from the
Golden Dawn's cockpit and messages Emily through Gracie: "I saw you
in the fourth row. You looked like yourself." Gracie adds Sharon's
note: bring the road atlas so she can argue with the route. Rachel
finds Emily afterward: "The Collective needs people who choose it.
Not as assets. As people who chose it." Emily: "Yes."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWENTY-FOUR — EARTH RETURN

The Golden Dawn breaks atmosphere over the Pacific at 0617. Gracie
runs sequencing: "Welcome home." Sharon is in the converted medical
bay, her augmented arm accommodated by Sam's harness setup. Taylor
is removed on the runway — two Federation task force agents, no
scene. He nods to Cole; Cole nods back. Taylor's quarters yield a
pocket watch: Klaus Verguld engraved inside the lid, coat of arms
with two rings over a star. DeMint takes Cole aside: Klaus Verguld
founded Alpha in 1991. Taylor was Klaus's man — not Marcus's. The
machine predates Marcus by a generation; Marcus has operated inside
it his entire life without knowing the full structure. Cole visits a
recovery facility north of San Diego. Emily is on the porch with
coffee and a road atlas penciled with new routes. "You look different.
Like something got lighter." Sharon is in the window chair: "Hi."
Bitboy: "Hi, you little bastard." Blackberry preserves. An argument
about the route through Prescott. Ricardo calls — Dani wants bowling.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHAPTER TWENTY-FIVE — THE BOWLING ALLEY

Cosmic Lanes: fog machines, black lights, carpet with a non-real star
chart. Sam found it; reservation for seven (Bitboy counts). Ricardo
proposes between frames — moissanite ring, full ethical sourcing and
cost breakdown provided, because Ricardo cannot help being exactly
who he is. Sam laughs and cries simultaneously. Yes. She finds a
compass emblem in his jacket pocket afterward — a Collective rank
patch she'd earned; he doesn't explain it. Sharon's first attempt
with her augmented arm sends the ball into the gutter at near-sonic
velocity; the music stops. She switches to her left arm: six plus
four, a spare. "Calibrating." Cole sets the copper-wire ring on the
table between them. "I don't think I need to replace it. I think
this is the one." Sharon puts it on. "Yes." Cole: "That wasn't a
question." / Sharon: "I know. It still applies." Bitboy destroys the
vending machine; everyone evacuates Cosmic Lanes. Parking lot.
California night.

EPILOGUE A: Marcus in Dubai, orange juice with four ice cubes. Klaus
Verguld appears — seventy-one, unhurried, three days already there.
"You built the cover. The structure continues." The machine was never
Marcus's. His grandfather built it; Marcus administered it.

EPILOGUE B: A Nevada high-desert town where the hospital died when
Alpha deprioritized the rural grid — seven people died there. Taylor
is placed there by Marcus, his new name leaked as payment for the
road attack he ordered without authorization. The machine disciplines
its own errors.

EPILOGUE C: Sharon on a Sequoia porch, late October, first snow on
the high peaks. Two ways of seeing — augmented eye versus her own.
A Collective droplet stencil on the porch railing. "It was always
in the reaching." She reaches. The last line of the book.

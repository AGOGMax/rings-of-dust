


Chapter 6









The plane's wheels bounced on the runway, and the speed brakes deployed with a bellowing whoosh. The sudden deceleration pressed Cole sideways in his chair. Ricardo calmly braced himself. Bitboy was jarred awake and, not recognizing his surroundings, screeched and howled, thrashing around in his chair. An attendant had a cart ready plane-side, and the corporal hoisted their bags onto it. Cole gripped one of the cart handles and dragged it along, with Bitboy clinging to his back like a living backpack. Armed guards escorted Cole and Ricardo through wide, mirrored doors reflecting the warm, setting sun.



Ricardo cracked a smile. &quot;A lot has changed since you took a sabbatical, bud. More than just the building.&quot;



Massive concrete pillars ascended several floors, supporting a high arched ceiling with industrial lights hanging above. Cole and Ricardo approached four individuals at the corridor's far end. At the head of the group loomed an elderly man in a tan general's uniform and hat, with a massive ring on each of his gnarled but welcoming hands.



&quot;Look what the cat dragged in. If it isn't Captain Cole Nova, the crown jewel of the Federation!&quot;



&quot;Chancellor DeMint,&quot; Cole said, &quot;I received your letter, sir.&quot;



The Chancellor nodded politely and returned his attention to Cole, gripping his hand like a vise. Cole forced a chuckle at the perfunctory greetings. To the right of Chancellor DeMint stood another man in casual juxtaposition with the Chancellor, wearing a crisp, white polo with a Federation Council Command logo, designer glasses, and white sneakers. He extended an easy greeting.



&quot;Vance Black. Operations. I hope y'all are rested; we have one week to get you fit for service.&quot;



Cole managed to extract his hand from the Chancellor's iron grip and hurriedly shook Vance's hand. Vance exuded confidence.



&quot;Now, let's get you presentable,&quot; DeMint said. &quot;Black will show you to your bunks. You'll want to take advantage of tonight; we begin at 0430 hours.&quot;



DeMint motioned to two young corpsmen, who came to collect Bitboy for kenneling and cleaning. Although Cole and Ricardo had managed to hose some of the gore off of him, the monkey still resembled a crime scene. Cole worried about parting with Bitboy but was also excited about being alone for a few hours.



&quot;I'll need you to read and sign these papers,&quot; Black said, handing Cole and Ricardo a small stack of legal documents.



Bitboy walked hand in hand with the two corpsmen down the hallway. Cole had to peel his eyes from the scene to read the documents in his hand. Something about upgraded AI, proprietary dermal, something or another. Both signed the last page after a quick skim. As Cole signed the last document, the pen's tip scratched against the paper with a finality that seemed to echo through the sterile room, and a small silver device embedded in the table activated. It released a cloud of microscopic particles that shimmered in the harsh overhead light. The nanopowder settled onto his skin, and for a moment, nothing happened. Then, the particles burrowed into his skin like countless tiny needles, each one a pinpoint of cold fire. The veins in his hand lit up, glowing faintly as the particles spread through his bloodstream, racing up his arm and across his body.



&quot;Welcome aboard, Mr. Nova,&quot; a voice said directly in his mind, startling him. It was cold and authoritative, unmistakably feminine, lacking any hint of warmth. &quot;I am IRiS, the Imbued Recursive Intelligence System. I will be your guide and assistant throughout your reactivated service.&quot;



Cole blinked, trying to shake off the strange sensation of the voice in his head. He nodded, feeling a slight tingle in his hand. He dismissed it as nerves. &quot;Thank you, IRiS. Let's get to work.&quot;



&quot;Well damn, if I had to have another voice in my head, IRiS, I sure am glad it's a beautiful woman.&quot; Ricardo's voice interrupted Cole's thoughts, his tone laced with dark humor.



Cole shot him a look, puzzled by Ricardo's reaction.



&quot;Officer Drummond and Chief Glenn have already arrived and gone through orientation for this mission. Please proceed to the decontamination chamber,&quot; IRiS instructed. &quot;We will begin your training immediately.&quot;

“Guess it’ll be a crew of 5” Cole noted.



Cole followed the directions, entering a sterile white chamber that hummed with the sound of hidden machinery. The walls glowed soft blue as he was sprayed with a disinfectant mist. Moments later, the door at the opposite end of the chamber opened, and he stepped through into a small briefing room where Ricardo was already seated.



Vance stood at the front. A man in his late 30s with “Taylor Glenn” embroidered on his jumpsuit and a deadpan expression sat at a circular table. Next to him a pleasant looking plump female also in her 30s “Sam Drummond” stitched on her name patch. A holographic display of Saturn and its surrounding space floating in the center console. &quot;Gentlemen, meet your crew for this mission.” Through a side door a private led Bitboi into the room. 

“Let's get down to business. We have a very tight window for this mission. The dark matter units we deployed are nearing the point where Saturn's gravity will claim them. We must retrieve them and any additional dark matter we can gather.&quot;



&quot;How long do we have?&quot; Cole asked, taking a seat as Bitboi Joined him in a partnering chair.



&quot;Nine and a half years from the initial deployment,&quot; Vance replied. &quot;We estimate just under two years to get there and position ourselves, a few months for the retrieval operations, and then two months to return with the dark matter using its energy to expedite our journey back.&quot;

&quot;Why is this mission so urgent now?&quot; Ricardo asked.

Vance's expression turned serious. &quot;Because all the dark matter vapor is about to be sucked into Saturn's gravitational pull. We can't let that happen. The retrieval window is closing fast. It’s a damn rapture.&quot;

Chancellor DeMint entered the room, his presence commanding immediate attention. &quot;Listen up, everyone. This mission is not just about retrieval. It's about securing the future of the Federation. We cannot afford any mistakes.&quot;

His voice was a blend of authority and control. &quot;You will follow IRiS's instructions to the letter. There is no room for error. Understood?&quot;

&quot;Yes, sir,&quot; Cole and Ricardo responded in unison. Sam and Taylor nodded obediently.

DeMint's eyes bore into them, ensuring his message was clear. &quot;Good. Now, gear up and be ready for launch. This is our one shot, and we cannot afford to miss it.&quot;

As they left the briefing room, Cole and Ricardo walked next to their new team mates. 

“Cole Nova.” he introduced himself as he stuck out his hand to Taylor who let it hang for a half a beat before extending his hand.

“Your reputation precedes you” Taylor said, the tone was not complimentary. He challenged Cole’s grip and looked past Cole with his eyes. Ricardo was instantly next to Sam.

“Hey there, looks like we're in good company” he said warmly.

A touch of color flashed in Sam's cheeks as she reached out and took Heavy’s hand “I read your profiles on the way here. You and Officer Nova have quite the resume, We look forward to assisting you in this vital mission.” She walked over to BitBoi. “Wow, the last remaining EM Cyber, so much cuter than I imagined” she smiled at Bitboi. “I’ll make sure you get a banana from the chow hall later”

“He’d probably prefer a bottle of perfume if you have one.” Cole quipped. They walked down the hall in a pack. Taylor made no attempt to greet Heavy and Bitboi which was ok with them.

As Cole walked he couldn't shake the feeling of impending doom mixed with a sense of determination. They had a mission, and the stakes had never been higher.

In their quarters, Cole and Ricardo prepared their gear in silence. The weight of the mission pressed heavily on their minds. Cole's thoughts drifted to IRiS, whose cold efficiency was starting to show signs of unexpected behavior. He noticed slight differences in how it interacted with him but brushed them aside. He couldn't afford to be distracted.

&quot;IRiS, give me a status update on our preparations,&quot; Cole said, breaking the silence.

&quot;All systems are operational, and the crew is ready,&quot; IRiS replied. The voice in his head was unsettlingly clear, as though someone were whispering directly into his ear.

In a dorm just across the glossy hall Data Technician Samantha Drummond and Crew Chief Taylor Glenn were unpacking their personal gear. Always the alpha, Taylor assumed the top bunk, throwing his ergonomic pillow and backpack on the mattress. His 20 years in the Federation had been tallied by promotions at or before the regular pace. He was built for this life, all except sleeping in zero gravity where had struggled to sleep without his head just so. Taylor was destined for greatness, he saw the federation as part of a grander vision. Ethics and morality had no place in competition and he was tapped by several military and alphabet groups while in college. They wanted to milk his ambition and cement him into upper middle management. None of them caught his interest until one day a man came to his sorority house and handed him a card. It simply read “Tailored Solutions” with an address underneath. Taylor smirked at the coincidence and before he could ask the man's name he was exiting the front door. Taylor watched as the man was ushered into the back of a black town car and whisked away.

Samantha had not gone by that name since she was old enough to express herself. Even her jumpsuits had “Sam” embroidered on them. She was a free and independent spirit, always going against the crowd. As a kid she was made fun of about her weight, which was the only thing she wished were “normal” about herself. Other than that she sought her own answers, her curiosity drew her towards science and biology. During her senior year she scored so high on the Federation Recruitment Entrance Exam ironically referred to as FREE the visiting officer wrote a body composition waiver on the spot. She had never been wooed by anybody or organization and it felt good to be seen. This was also her ticket to attend the federation’s Science and Technology District. It was THE pinnacle of S.T.E.M. institutes despite its unfortunate acronym (STD). There she studied the work of Dr Stephen Conrad and his work with Nanoparticles. She struggled through basic training but breezed through the class work. She was always good with coding and computers but found she had a real knack for firewalls and white hat hacking. She always felt out of place in this world of conformity and rigidity and didn’t really subscribe to the goals of the federations “machine”. She had always felt deep down that if you had a talent or skill that others lacked that it was only natural to use that gift to help others. Maybe it was because people had always been so graceless with her appearance that she wanted to add balance to the world, compensation. In this cookie cutter barracks she missed her punk rock and psychedelic posters she had at home in her apartment. She wished there were a hidey hole here. One where she could keep her secret relics and patches, her stencils.

With IRiS guiding them through all the new tech and innovations of the spaceship the week of training and instructions flew by. Bitboi was the only member of the crew that had not been inoculated with the electronics spore. The four humans processed and retained more information than any point in their lives. After one particularly strenuous module Ricardo turned to Cole and in his best Keanu Reeves impersonation said, “I know kung Fu!”

As Cole rolled out of his bunk, his hand beating the button on his alarm before it could go off and break the silence in the room of three. IRis had their schedule available and would wake him up directly but he still wasn’t completely used to integrating “her” into his life. His sleep was mixed with fits of tossing and turning even though he knew he needed the rest. He could never sleep the night before a launch. I guess some things never change, he thought to himself. Now that he was up he could hear the buzz of people outside. As he entered the hall, everyone was walking 25% faster than normal. He carried his bathroom kit to the bathroom. Last hot shower for the next few months. 

After a forgettable breakfast the crew entered the main operations room where Taylor and Sam were already busy with pre-launch preparations. The room buzzed with the hum of machinery and the faint sounds of the ship's systems coming online.

The crew grabbed their gear and personal belongings and headed out to the launch pad. 

The spaceship loomed on the launchpad, its sleek, metallic frame gleaming. Ascending the ladder, Cole's footsteps were echoed by Ricardo, Taylor, Sam, and Bitboy. Inside the spacecraft the crew stowed their belongings in clearly marked bins and compartments. Cole sank into the command chair, enveloped by the silence and stillness that belied the chaos outside. He surveyed the control panel. Closing his eyes, he visualized the forthcoming ordeal, the suit's familiar pressure points, and the sequence of actions that would propel them into the unknown. But as the ship began to come alive with the hum of systems and the soft beeping of diagnostics, an unwelcome guest invaded Cole's solitude—fear. A visceral, gut-wrenching fear that constricted his chest and set his nerves on edge. Memories of past failures, the echo of screams, and the searing pain of loss flooded back. The cockpit, once a place of control, now felt like a trap, closing around him, threatening to suffocate him under the weight of his dread.



Ricardo and Bitboy clambered into the cabin. Ricardo strapped himself in while Cole helped Bitboy into a seat next to his own. 

Ricardo: (laughing) &quot;Hey Bitboi, ready for another adventure?&quot;

Bitboi: (chirping in response, extending its mantis blades briefly)

Amid the cacophony of beeping systems and the soft whirl of machinery, Taylor and Sam entered the cabin, moving with focused intent. With Cole in the center, the crew was complete. Bitboy, with his usual curious gaze, looked around, sensing the tension but not fully grasping the human apprehension that filled the air. Ever the stoic guardian, Ricardo checked and double-checked his harness, offering silent nods of encouragement to Cole. Taylor and Sam, embodying a blend of youth and experience, meticulously went through their pre-launch rituals, their hands steady as they toggled switches and scanned the screens before them. Their precise and deliberate actions wove a tapestry of collective endeavor—a unity of purpose transcending the sum of their parts. Together, they vocalized their readiness. Their voices converged in a harmony of shared resolve and anticipation.



&quot;Captain Nova checking in. We are locked in and ready for pre-flight check.&quot;



&quot;Initiating pre-flight check,&quot; Iris reported in their heads.



&quot;Comms online,&quot; Taylor said.



&quot;Vitals within range,&quot; IRiS said. &quot;Golden Dawn online. All systems functioning.&quot;



Bitboy offered a grunt.



&quot;All systems are green,&quot; Iris said.



In the control room, DeMint addressed the sleep deprived  engineers. &quot;Everybody got their coffee? It's going to be a long day.&quot; He touched the intercom. &quot;Cole, do you have anything to say to our engineers?&quot;



&quot;Thank you all for everything you guys have done to get us to this point. This is for everybody who didn't get to be with us.&quot;



The engineers cheered.



One of the senior engineers in the back of the room started the chant, quietly at first, &quot;Jack-pot, Jack-pot,&quot; and the others joined in, louder and louder.



Vance stepped up to the microphone. &quot;I think the feeling is mutual, Captain Nova. Good work, good work everyone. This is just the beginning.&quot;



Vance paused for effect, then said: &quot;Golden Dawn, you are clear for takeoff.&quot;



Cole's hands, steady and determined, hovered over the console, ready to initiate the launch sequence. Ricardo and Bitboy, secured in their seats, braced for the imminent thrust. The cabin was a symphony of anticipation, each member acutely aware of their role in the dance between man and machine. With a final glance at his crew, Cole completed the ignition sequence.



&quot;In the pipe,&quot; Cole said. &quot;Five by five.&quot;



The engines, dormant giants beneath them, awakened with a primal growl, escalating into a thunderous roar that resonated through the hull. The vibration was a tangible force, gripping them with invisible hands as the Golden Dawn rebelled against the Earth's hold. The ascent began, a relentless push that melded them into their seats, the force of gravity asserting its dominance even as they sought to escape its bounds. The cabin, bathed in the glow of instrument panels, became a capsule of hope and fear hurtling towards the unknown. Outside, the atmosphere clawed at the ship. The maelstrom of resistance that tested the integrity of their vessel. The Golden Dawn responded with grace and purpose. It sliced through the air.

Cole, his gaze locked on the readouts before him, felt a surge of adrenaline. Every sensation was intimately familiar and utterly terrifying. As they broke through the atmosphere, the resistance fell away, replaced by the profound silence of space. The darkness outside the viewport was absolute. The Golden Dawn, now free from the shackles of gravity, glided into the void. Its engines a soft hum in the quietude of space. A team bound by duty looked ahead to the challenges and wonders that awaited them, their journey into the unknown just beginning.

With Iris now following the flight path and the G-forces of launch a memory now the crew were free to unbuckle and detach themselves from their seats. Each were guided by Iris in their heads to their stations. For Ricardo this was an irritating echo of what he knew. For Sam iris was a reassuring echo of her movements. As Taylor stood at the communication station, he was engrossed in the ship's diagnostics, his hands moving with practiced ease over the controls.

&quot;Taylor,&quot; Cole said, announcing his presence with a gentle cough. &quot;How’s everything coming along?”

Taylor turned, his eyes scanning Cole and Ricardo with a mix of curiosity and skepticism. &quot;Captain Nova, Ricardo.” His hand ran unconsciously along the Smartcom in his pocket. Hoping he’d remembered to turn the vibrations when messages came in. 



Sam was relaxed at her console surrounded by a nest of monitors. Her eyes sparkled with intelligence, and she had an air of confidence that put Cole at ease. Sam's lighthearted approach balanced the intensity of the situation, making the grueling preparations more bearable.



This being a federation mission, no one thought to check for other spacecraft in the area. An alpha corp cargo container cruised along the inside a large empty cavern aside from a few crates full of weapons. 

Just out of radar another ship scuttled along, this one much smaller. An old decommissioned transport shuttle abandoned by the failed Republic Of East Asia. It was filled to the brim with radio amps for long range communication. Inside was a crew of three. Their handsewn insignias collected the frost from their breath. 

One of the men turned to the others

“Are we considered drops or snowflakes at this temp?”

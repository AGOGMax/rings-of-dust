


Chapter 1







Cole Nova stepped into the dim confines of the starship Polaris's abandoned storage bay. His presence, typically filling the room with undisputed authority, fell silently under the constant hum emitted from the engine room directly below. 

He crept along to verify each of the 14 harvesters in each row was operational. Luminescent bands traced the contours of each slender monolith, softly pulsing in unison. Green equals good. Under his hastily adorned space suit, the inner stitching on his &quot;JackPot&quot; patch rubbed a valley of irritation into Cole's firm chest. A gift from &quot;his crew,&quot; thus more unique than the many insignia he wore at parties. This one Cole treasured. The itch of his rough stitch filled him with pride that was beginning to twist into fear. 

With thoughts of being Saturn's next meal, he continued to inspect row after row. The green light cast an otherworldly glow over Cole's features. The storage bay remained still. Like a tether, Cole felt the ship's vibration through the soles of his boots.

Meanwhile, in a neighboring ship, the Falcon, Ricardo &quot;Heavy&quot; Corazon, and BitBoi remained hard at work. The day's loot-filled the Falcon's cargo hold with various harvested substances bound for Alpha Corps' space station. Heavy glanced around the cargo hold cluttered with crates and equipment. BitBoi, with his six mechanical limbs, moved with practiced precision, securing the straps on a stack of ore and harvesters.

&quot;BitBoi, make sure those crates are secure,&quot; Heavy called out.

BitBoi's mechanical appendages whirred softly as he tightened the straps. The dim light highlighted the disorderly array of tools scattered around him, yet his meticulous care shone through his controlled, thoughtful movement. 

&quot;Alright, let's get this shipment to the depot,” Heavy said while he checked the manifest, nodding in satisfaction. “We're running a tight schedule.&quot;

BitBoi quickly rechecked the straps, ensuring every crate was locked in place. Though cluttered, the Falcon's cargo hold reflected the symbiotic relationship between the two. Heavy's brute strength and BitBoi's agile precision created an equilibrium. The walls around them bore the marks of countless missions, scratches, and dents telling stories of their ventures.

As Cole moved deeper into the storage bay, the green glow gradually gave way to shadows. The rhythmic hum surrounding him grew fainter. Cole's steps slowed in the face of his target.A gentle, yet ominous, red glow. The air grew colder, and a faint thought of his sister tickled his spine. His pulse quickened to a thrumb with the pulsing of the anomalous harvester before him. The red glow intensified as he drew nearer, dancing across the metallic walls. Cole wavered in the absence of his crew, all of whom—including the captain—were now confined to their quarters to conserve energy for the ship's systems.Over the last few months, several ships had been tasked with collecting samples from the Rings of Saturn. Much like crab pots in earth’s ocean, these scientific missions would release a string of containers lined with various salts, and gas, dust, and ice would condense into liquids and chunks of minerals. During these missions, crews began experimenting with a new substance that held the properties of neither gas nor liquid–DarkX was more of a plasma. An obsidian thorn grown from the fabric of space itself, the anomaly thrummed with a life of its own, a pulsating heart of shadow and light. Cole was tasked with securing a sample ahead of the fleet's return. He watched light bend and fracture across the rigid incandescent surface of the slow-moving substance. He opened the DarkX harvesting container,not to touch but to understand, and as his gloved hand neared the surface of the crystal, its tendrils stretched and twitched towards him.

His mind snapped to the past few days. Once a mystery, the crew's odd behavior now seemed a direct response to these crystals' silent yet pervasive influence, subtly altering the crew's perceptions and actions. Cole's understanding deepened as he recognized that a tide ebbed and flowed within the crew, much like in this darkness made visible.

&quot;Hey, Gracie,&quot; Cole said. &quot;You seeing this?&quot;

&quot;First Officer Nova, I would be happy to help,&quot; the ship's AI said. &quot;I have initiated preliminary scans. Please proceed with caution. The crystalline structure is highly irregular.&quot;

&quot;Yeah. It's pretty wild in here. I think suiting up was a good idea after all,&quot; Cole said with a narrow glass collection tube in hand. &quot;Gracie, let's see what these crystals are made of. Any theories?&quot;

&quot;I'm checking the databases now, First Officer Nova. 

&quot;How's the crew? Oh, and Gracie, please review our previous conversations from your memory.&quot; Cole said.

&quot;Absolutely, Cole. I'm up to speed and monitoring crew vitals.I’ve detected elevated stress levels across all crew members, and, Cole,” Gracie said. &quot;There is no record of this compound in any database I can access.&quot;

The anomaly danced like the shadow of a firelight as Cole led the narrow glass collection tube toward the crystalline entity. The gentle hum of engines and the soft whir of life support systems drew back to observe this critical event. Cole, too, watched as the crystal's intricate patterns seized into and out of focus, like a dark palm frond fluttering between dimensions. The hums and whirs of the ship undulated into a surreal soundscape while his is heart beat a slow, deliberate knocking against his composure.

&quot;Cole,&quot; Gracie said, &quot;in its raw state, DarkX is extremely volatile. Any instability could trigger a chain reaction beyond our control.&quot;

The red glow intensified. Once inside, it grew across the interior surface, the way roots crawl through the soil. The structure pulsed erratically. Cole's breath caught in his throat as he tightened his grip on the tube. He secured the sample and sealed the harvester..

&quot;Gracie, keep monitoring the containment unit. No matter how minor, alert me immediately.”.

&quot;Understood, Cole. Continuous monitoring initiated.”

With practiced grace, Cole secured the tube to a specialized compartment designed for hazardous materials on the waist of his suit. He tugged the compartment to ensure it was tightly fastened and isolated from the environment.



Each step carried him further from the anomaly source and closer to the ship's heart, where his crew awaited his return. Cole quickened his steps, driven as if walking up the stairs in the darkness of night. The dimly lit bay felt as if it were stalking behind him, taunting him.  With the storage bay biting at his back, Cole's mind raced with plans and contingencies. Isolation protocols were temporary; understanding the anomaly's true nature was his mission and thus his priority. The safety of his ship and the lives under his impromptu command depended on the actions he was about to take.With a determined breath, Cole set his sights on the medical bay as he neared the imposing doors leading out of the storage bay.

&quot;Cole, I’m detecting an intermittent yet increasing energy surge from the harvester you sampled.&quot;

The mention spiked his concern. 

&quot;Hail the Falcon. Shit. Alert the crew to take emergency precautions, well, more emergency precautions. And keep trying the hooligans. Cole's voice tightened with a mix of frustration and concern.” Hail the Falcon and Horus, and tell the crew in Medbay to drink some strong coffee.&quot;

Broadcast ship-wide in her most  clear, authoritative tone, Gracie did her best to carefully alert the crew: &quot;Attention all crew: Emergency protocols 11, 22, and 33 are now in effect. Please proceed to your designated emergency stations

The bay doors slammed shut. The ship's internal lighting shifted, bathing corridors and rooms in a pulsating red light. The hum of the engines halted. The air was humid with impending danger. Throughout the vessel, doors sealed automatically with a pneumatic whisper. Along the corridors, emergency guide lights flickered to life. A network of luminescent breadcrumbs led the way to emergency stations and escape pods. The crew moved with a sense of urgency. Their faces were stricken by a mix of practiced resolve and underlying anxiety. Trained for emergencies but seldom confronted with their immediacy, they navigated the shifting landscape of their vessel. The familiar becoming less and less so in the shadow of an unknown emergency. Monitors across the ship flashed with updates and instructions.

Again Gracie’s voice carried through the growing chaos, echoing throughout the ship among the fear and dedication of highly trained Federation personnel: &quot;Attention all crew: Emergency protocols 11, 22, and 33 are in effect. Proceed to your designated emergency stations.” 

Brilliant incandescent light erupted from the sampled harvester, engulfing the bay in a tidal wave of radiance. In an instant, the ship transformed from careful chaos to outright disaster. Time slowed. The shockwave tore toward Cole through the last remaining atmosphere within the crumbling ship. The explosion , a cacophony of unyielding force, fractured reality into a vortex of light and sound. A deep, resonating roar echoed through the shivering corridors. More alarms joined the chorus, their wails rising to a fevered pitch. Cole, caught in the ferocious heat and pressure from the unyielding wave of unimaginable energy., was flung, weightlessly hopeless, toward the sealed bay doors.

Back on the Falcon, Ricardo sat frozen while he watched the Polaris transform into a wasteland devoured by a sudden, blinding flash. The Falcon rocked violently as the dead and debris reached them, impacting the Falcon’s outer shell. Heavy grabbed the nearest support beam. BitBoi's limbs anchored him to the floor.

&quot;What the hell is this?&quot; Heavy yelled over what sounded like an oversized hail storm. .

BitBoi's sensors whirred.Heavy's heart pounded in his chest. &quot;We need to get over there, now!&quot;

BitBoi was already at the controls, plotting a course. The Falcon's engines roared to life as they veered towards the Polaris, dodging debris that littered the space between them. As they approached, the full extent of the devastation became apparent. The once sleek and formidable Polaris was now a mangled wreck, its hull breached and flames licking at the ruptured sections.

&quot;Get the rescue gear ready,&quot; Heavy said. &quot;We need to find survivors.&quot;



Cole focused on a faint whistle coming from a lattice of cracks forming across his helmet's visor and a faint whistle. The ship's skeleton warped and fractured under the strain. Metal alloys engineered to endure the harshness of space folded and twisted like paper caught in a gale. Fuel cells ruptured in an obscene display, blooming into fiery orbs that consumed everything in their path. Jettisoned from the ship, Cole became an island of stillness amid the chaos. He floated untethered in the violent disarray around him. His eyes mirrored the devastation. His ship—his crew—was torn apart and fed to the void. Flames and metal shards danced a ghastly silent ballet all around him. Light flashed in violent, soundless spasms, illuminating a fragmented wasteland.

Cole's voice emerged, barely audible in his helmet. &quot;All crew report,&quot; he uttered into the void, each syllable seeming to float away, swallowed by the enormity of the void. Again, he tried, &quot;Triage team report,&quot; his words dissipating into the vacuum. Unanswered. Leaving an almost tangible silence in which Cole could hear his blood flowing.

A whisper of desperation followed, &quot;Gracie…Anyone?&quot;

In the overwhelming quiet that followed, Cole activated the recorder using the control unit on his left wrist. His voice echoed a somber determination in the confined space of his helmet.

&quot;Mission Log – Final Entry 2034,&quot; Cole's voice carried the weight of the moment, clear and unwavering despite the chaos around him. &quot;This is First Officer Cole Nova. Our ship has sustained critical damage following an unexpected explosion in the storage bay. I've managed to secure a sample of the anomaly. It may be the key to understanding what's happened. You should find it on my body. All efforts were taken to ensure the crew's safety, execute emergency protocols, and stabilize the situation to the best of our abilities. However, it appears I'm the only survivor.&quot;

He paused, collecting his thoughts before recording personal notes. Speaking of his crewmates, &quot;If this is found, let it be known that Ricardo, 'Heavy Hitter,' was more than our second officer; he is one of the best Astros I've had the honor of serving with. His inexhaustible humor and strength are the cement that holds a good crew together, even in the darkest hours. And then there's Bitboy, whose contributions were as invaluable as they were unusual. With an agility and problem-solving ability that surpasses all expectations, he performs impossible tasks for the rest of us. His presence was a constant reminder of the extraordinary lengths this mission has gone to in exploring the unknown.&quot;

Cole's voice softened affectionately as he addressed his sister, &quot;Emily, the guiding light in my life. Distance and time mean nothing against the bond we share. Look to the stars; I will always watch over and guide you. Keep our dreams alive, and remember, you're always in my heart. I love you.&quot;

With the message complete, Cole sent it into the darkness, watching as if he could see the signal weave through the graveyard before him.

Against the backdrop of the starship's fractured body, a surge of deliberate motion broke the stillness. A single lifeline maneuvering with precise, purposeful movements. The vehicle weaved through the floating carnage. Inside, Ricardo sat and worked alongside a chimpanzee, Bitboy. Somewhat more unusual, Bitboy operated his six limbs in a symphony of coordinated movement. Under their expert control, the vehicle danced through the maelstrom of floating wreckage and the disfigured bodies of the crew. Charred and jagged twisted metal beams floated alongside remnants of shattered consoles and unrecognizable equipment. Bitboy's six biomechanical limbs hummed with exertion. His eyes darted between the control panel's readouts and the chaos beyond their viewport, each glance a rapid assessment. His movements were less those of a pilot and more akin to a conductor orchestrating a symphony. Shattered fragments of the ship drifted like ghostly apparitions, reflecting the golden light of Saturn's rings. Ricardo, firmly at the helm, embodied a navigator's intuition. His hands, firmly gripping the controls, steered the craft with an innate understanding of its capabilities. Bits of debris spun lazily. Bodies, still and lifeless, moved with a macabre grace. As they closed the distance to Cole, the debris thinned, revealing the drifting figure of their comrade, a lone survivor amidst the void.

The void held Cole. Its weightless embrace infected him. The very absence of force became a presence that engulfed him. Suspended in the silent expanse, Cole sank into hopelessness, and a chasm grew inside him. His oxygen supply whistled through the cracks in his faceplate. No tether. No rescue. No hope. He sought solace in his training—the rigorous preparations to steal him against the unforgiving. Yet, as he floated adrift, he confronted a stark truth: humans muster an indomitable spirit within their engineered havens. Stripped of these constructs, cast into the vast, indifferent expanse, humanity flounders, unmoored and vulnerable. At this moment, Cole watched the emptiness of space silently claim its prize as it relegated the ship's debris and the bodies of his crew to an eternity among Saturn's miraculous rings. In this profound isolation, Cole spiraled. So much of his existence was spent fortifying against the elements, against each other, and against the unknown. Yet here he was, without any semblance of stronghold within or out. His heart raced as the void's lesson on his overwhelming insignificance. His feet, fingers, and eyes tingled between each commanding beat of his heart. He watched, as if from a distance, the slow, aimless waltz of debris and the remains of his crew. The stark beauty of the rings, untouchable and remote. His sudden isolation pressed in on him. The suit's internal temperature and oxygen supply indicators flashed warnings. With the infinite stretching out inexorably before him, Cole's thoughts drifted to what anchored him to life—his memories, dreams, and humanity's essence. Emily's face, laughter, and smile flickered like distant stars in the dark.

A thoughtful glint caught his eye. A sliver of movement that arched and weaved with purpose. The one object that defied the slow twisting of the debris littered among the rock, dust, and ice. A shuttle, its form barely discernible. Yet as hope sparked, so too did a shadow of doubt. The specter of isolation clung to Cole even as the shuttle emerged as a beacon in the consuming darkness. How could they see him, a lone soul adrift amid the wreckage of dreams? The vast canvas of space seemed indifferent to his plight. Yet there it was—a lifeline cast across the abyss. His resolve faltered, his hand hovering over the thruster controls, paralyzed. Fear and hope tangled within him, every heartbeat echoing the shuttle's advance. He pressed down on the thrusters. Their gentle hum added to the soft whistle of his oxygen supply, and sneaking away continued. His body remained sharply numb, but shortly after, the distance separating Cole from the shuttle dwindled to almost nothing.

The shuttle's side hatch swung open slowly, deliberately. From within, gloved hands stretched into the emptiness. The sight stirred something deep within Cole, a well of emotions dammed by fear and desperation. Contact! A rescuer's glove brushed against Cole's. A spark of life against the cold. The grip was unsure at first, fingers sliding across the slick fabric of his suit. Panic surged in Cole's chest. The terrifying possibility of slipping away felt all too real. Another hand joined. Its grip was more substantial and confident. Cole's world teetered on the edge. Then he felt a pull, a physical tether back to life. His breath hitched, eyes locked with his rescuer's through the visor. They drew him into the shuttle's embrace. The shift from the void's chill to the shuttle's warmth jarred Cole. Frantic voices wrapped around him. Cole laid still. Still floating but held now by a simulacrum of safety. Air, rich and sweet, flooded his lungs. The hum of the cabin, a sound of safety and civilization, washed him of panic. Ricardo's and Bitboy's faces, seen through a blur of tears, shone with relief and warmth. Their smiles lit the darkness.

Behind them in the cargo compartment were stacked and strapped-down harvesters of different substances bound for the A Corps space station and the depot, where they would be loaded on larger cargo shuttles and sent to Earth.

&quot;Are any of these filled with DarkX?&quot; he demanded.

&quot;Most of them are top priority on the manifest,&quot; Ricardo stated.

&quot;It's not safe!&quot; he urged, trying to rip the sample tube from his pocket like a stick of lit dynamite.

&quot;What are you talking about? The ore and sludge we're packing is sealed tight, even wearing a seatbelt,&quot; Heavy gestured toward the tie-down straps.

&quot;I took this sample from a harvester right before it destroyed our entire ship!&quot;

&quot;Why the Hell would you open a harvester and play with that creepy pudding?&quot;

&quot;I was following orders.&quot;

&quot;Cole, I know this isn't the time, but sometimes you gotta play dumb and pretend your radio is broken.&quot;

Cole regained his composure and stood transfixed by the devastation outside the shuttle's viewport. The remnants of his starship twisted and turned amidst the cosmic whirl of debris orbiting Saturn alongside them. In the shuttle's glossy viewport, the torment in his powder-blue eyes stared back with a compelling argument on the meaninglessness of life. He saw the same veil of loss that shrouded him, enveloping Ricardo and Bitboy. The shared burden of survival. In this charged silence, Cole faced an unyielding truth: the relentless drive towards the unknown, the insatiable hunger for discovery and dominion exacted a toll far more significant than anyone could anticipate. Any journey paved with ambition and the quest for knowledge–often left its deepest scars on those in the vanguard. At the same time, those who commanded demanded isolation from real risk. At this moment, Cole understood the true cost of curiosity.
